# Adam — Character Sheet

## Narrative Role
Adam is a **minor but thematically critical character** introduced in Chapter 4. He does not recur as a POV character after this chapter.

His function is to:
- demonstrate **corrupted bearing** in action
- contrast with Nick and Mike’s empowerment arcs
- show how Butch Bear’s ideology recruits willingly, not violently

Adam is not special. He is **representative**.

---

## Core Concept
Adam embodies an **identity plateau**:
- he has achieved the body and desirability he was promised
- he is socially and sexually fluent
- he is exhausted by constant self-authorship and negotiation

His transformation is not driven by desperation or ignorance, but by **recognition and relief**.

---

## Physical Appearance (Canon)

- **Race:** white
- **Age:** mid-to-late 20s
- **Height:** average
- **Build:**
  - upper-body dominant
  - lean, athletic, low body fat
  - visibly trained chest, shoulders, arms
  - slightly under-massed compared to idealized circuit-party physiques
- **Overall presence:** attractive, legible, but not dominant

## Face & Hair
- **Face:** softer, slightly boyish; authority not yet settled
- **Eyes:** grey; calm, observant, understated
- **Hair:**
  - short, clean sides
  - longer textured top
  - falls into his face when sweaty
  - habitually pushed back between sets

## Skin & Grooming
- **Skin tone:** light with a year-round tan
- **Grooming:** clean-shaven
- **Piercing:** small, understated silver septum ring (only anchor detail)

---

## Clothing & Visual Signals

- **Gym shirt:**
  - black, worn-in
  - features a faded white emblem
  - emblem is sharp, geometric, and abstract
  - visually suggests a stylized animal snout reduced to hard lines and symmetry

**Narrative function of the shirt:**
- signals kink literacy and subcultural fluency
- not consciously worn as a signal by Adam
- plausibly read by Bill as familiarity with power-exchange dynamics

---

## Psychology & Motivation

Adam is:
- disciplined
- consistent
- socially experienced
- comfortable being seen

He is **not** insecure about his body or desirability.

His dissatisfaction comes from:
- constant self-monitoring
- endless micro-decisions
- negotiating identity, effort, and meaning

What he wants (implicitly):
- relief from authorship
- fewer decisions
- externally imposed structure that feels earned

---

## Relationship to Consent

Adam is capable of consent and recognizes what is being offered.

However:
- his consent is **explicit but incomplete**
- he agrees to a familiar symbolic framework (a fetish dynamic)
- he does not understand the metaphysical consequences

This ambiguity is intentional and core to the chapter.

---

## End State

By the end of the chapter:
- Adam has undergone corrupted bearing
- his body and identity feel **finished** rather than expanded
- hierarchy feels natural, not imposed

Adam’s story ends once his function is established.
