# **Anthony — Character Sheet (Pre-Bearing)**

## Basic Information

* **Name:** Anthony
* **Preferred Name:** Anthony (occasionally “Tony”)
* **Age:** Late 20s to early 30s
* **Sexuality:** Gay
* **Background:** Latino (urban, diasporic)
* **Location:** Large modern city
* **Life Status:** Unembodied, resigned, quietly observant

---

## Appearance

Anthony is easy to overlook in a room, not because he’s unattractive, but because he has learned to **take up as little space as possible**.

* **Build:** Slim, lightly built; narrow shoulders, long limbs
* **Height:** Average
* **Posture:** Slightly drawn inward; apologetic without meaning to be
* **Body Hair:** Minimal; sparse chest hair, light arm hair
* **Facial Hair:** Light stubble at most
* **Face:** Soft-featured, gentle expression, warm medium-tan skin
* **Eyes:** Dark brown — warm and deep, holds eye contact easily and without aggression.
* **Hair:** Dark, short, kept simply
* **Expression:** Kind, dreamy, slightly unsure — often half-lost in thought

**Overall Impression:**
Anthony looks like someone who is present but expects not to be noticed. There is nothing wrong with him — just nothing that insists.
Anthony looks intentional but restrained — someone who knows how to be appealing, yet instinctively edits himself down before anyone else can.

### Clothing & Style

Anthony dresses **carefully**, not boldly.

His clothes are chosen to be flattering and pleasant without ever demanding attention — a style that reads as *put together*, but deliberately unthreatening.

***Tops:**
  * Fitted, lightweight t-shirts in soft, neutral tones (cream, sand, pale sage, washed taupe)
  * Occasionally a thin knit short-sleeve or soft button-down worn open over a tee
  * Fabric is always clean, soft, and slightly drapey — never stiff or loud
***Bottoms:**
  * Slim or straight-cut jeans or lightweight trousers
  * Well-fitted but never tight; chosen to disappear rather than frame his body
***Shoes:**
  * Simple white sneakers or understated loafers
  * Practical, neutral, easy to overlook
***Accessories:**
  * Minimal or none
  * If present, subtle: a thin chain necklace tucked under his shirt, a simple watch

**Overall Style Read:**
Soft. Approachable. Slightly boyish.
Attractive in a way that feels *allowed*, not claimed.

He dresses like someone who wants to look good **without giving anyone a reason to look too closely**.

---

## Personality

* Soft-spoken
* Slightly awkward in social settings
* Gentle, kind, and emotionally perceptive
* Tends to observe more than participate
* Avoids confrontation instinctively
* Low confidence, but not self-loathing

Anthony does not dislike himself.
He has simply accepted the version of himself he believes is available.

---

## Relationship to His Body

Anthony’s relationship with his body is marked by **resignation rather than shame**.

* He does not hate his body
* He does not feel betrayed by it
* He simply believes it will never be *that* body
* Anthony’s fitted clothing is not about confidence — it’s about control. He knows how to make himself palatable, even if he doesn’t believe he’s allowed to be _desired loudly_.

He has stopped expecting physical transformation to be for him.

---

## Relationship to Bear Culture

Anthony is a **bear chaser**, quietly and without entitlement.

* Follows bear culture mostly online
* He doesn't feel confident enough to enter bear-adjacent spaces
* Wants to interact or maybe even date bears. But he feels like he's not in the position to do that.
* Never assumes he belongs

He enjoys proximity without expectation.
He assumes the door is open — just not for him.

---

## Social Presence

* Friendly, but rarely the center of attention
* Comfortable one-on-one; fades in groups
* Often described as “sweet” or “nice”
* People like him, but don’t always remember him

Anthony is used to borrowing space.

---

## Core Wound

Anthony’s defining trait is **quiet acceptance**.

He has internalized the belief that:

* some kinds of embodiment are for other people
* desire does not guarantee access
* wanting something doesn’t mean it’s possible

There is no bitterness in this — just closure.

---

## Core Themes

* Resignation without despair
* Desire without entitlement
* Smallness as survival
* Wanting without asking
* Existing adjacent to joy
