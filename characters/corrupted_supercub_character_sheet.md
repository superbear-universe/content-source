# Corrupted Supercub — Character Sheet

## Basic Information
- **Hero Name (Corrupted):** Supercub (designation retained by Bill)
- **Civilian Identity:** Nick
- **Status:** Captured / Corrupted / Active Brotherhood Asset
- **Tier in Brotherhood:** Uncategorized — occupies a unique position directly beneath Bill
- **Designation:** Lover Boy (Bill's term; reflects Nick's function and perceived status)
- **Narrative Role:** Weapon, trophy, and unwilling army engine

---

## Overview

Corrupted Supercub is what happens when bearing-based superpowers meet forced corruption at full scale.

Nick is not a Boy. He is not a Sir. He is something Bill has never encountered before and has no existing category for — a bearer with hero-level powers, emotionally open enough to have been trapped, and strong enough that standard Brotherhood methods cannot hold him alone.

The corrupted suit is Bill's solution. It is unprecedented in the Brotherhood's history.

Nick wears it.
Nick cannot remove it.
Nick is still in there.

---

## How the Capture Happened

### Step One — Going Alone
Nick learns Evan's location through his own investigation. Mike tells him he is not ready for a solo operation against Butch Bear specifically — not because he doubts Nick's power, but because he correctly identifies Nick's emotional attachment to Evan as an exploitable vulnerability. Nick hears it as condescension. He goes while Mike is asleep.

### Step Two — The Fight
Nick enters Brotherhood territory and finds Evan. Seeing his corrupted friend is immediately destabilizing — Nick hesitates where he should act, tries to reach Evan emotionally rather than treat him as a threat. Brotherhood Boys are present, blocking exits and creating pressure, but they are not the primary obstacle.

Bill arrives.

The fight that follows is between two bearers with comparable raw strength. But the comparison ends there. Bill has lived with these powers far longer. He fights efficiently, precisely, reading Nick's movements and exploiting every hesitation. Nick is strong but unpolished — he has never faced someone who matches him physically and has no framework for it.

Bill's dominion works on Nick throughout the fight, not controlling him but compressing his emotional state — the sight of Evan, the unfamiliar opponent, the weight of having come alone — all of it narrows Nick's decision space faster than he can compensate. He is fighting at a fraction of his capacity while Bill is fully himself.

Nick puts up a real fight. He lands hits. He does not go down easily or quickly.

But Bill wins.

### Step Three — The Wristband
With Nick restrained and exhausted, Bill clasps the Brotherhood wristband onto his wrist personally. Unhurried. Like it was inevitable.

The wristband is an enchanted artifact carrying concentrated corrupted bearing energy. On a civilian it would begin transformation immediately. On Supercub it does something different: it suppresses his bearing-based powers, draining his remaining strength and durability faster than he can compensate.

Nick is taken.

---

## The Corrupted Suit

### What It Is
The Corrupted Suit is a full-body latex garment engineered by Brotherhood resources using a concentrated compound derived directly from Bill's own power. It is unprecedented — designed specifically because standard methods are insufficient to control a bearer with superhero-level powers.

Unlike a chest harness, the suit is total. It covers and conditions the entire body simultaneously, functioning as a full-contact control artifact rather than a localized conduit. It does not suppress Nick's powers entirely — it redirects them, bending his bearing energy toward Bill's purposes while keeping him functional as an asset.

### How It Is Applied
Nick is held in a Brotherhood cell, chained and weakened by the wristband. Bill enters alone.

What Bill releases into the cell comes from inside his own body — a thick, dark substance produced organically and released through his mouth. Within the Brotherhood it has no formal name. It is simply what it is: Bill's corrupted bearing energy made physical, concentrated, alive in its orientation. His body produces a specialised version — more potent than anything a Boy can generate — reserved for exceptional circumstances. Nick is the first and only target it has ever been used on.

Bill does not frame this as violation. In his worldview he is giving something of himself to something worthy of it. That belief does not change what it is.

The substance moves with deliberate, almost sentient purpose once released. It doesn't attack. It covers.

Starting at Nick's feet, it spreads upward across his existing suit, absorbing it rather than replacing it. It finds every surface, every seam, moving slowly enough that Nick feels every inch of it. His suit doesn't disappear — it becomes something else. The colors drain. The warmth leaves. The material hardens into a second skin.

By the time it reaches his face Nick knows exactly what is happening and cannot stop it.

Bill is present for this. He directs it. It is a deliberate act of claiming, not a mechanical procedure.

The fact that the suit originates from inside Bill's body is not incidental. It makes the corruption intimate in a way that harnesses and wristbands are not. Nick wears something that came from Bill. That detail does not need to be stated explicitly in the story to do its work.

### Visual Appearance After Application

Nick's Supercub suit is transformed into a **tight, shiny black and gray latex version** of his original suit. The silhouette is recognizably his — same build, same cape, same emblem placement — but corrupted in every detail.

- **Primary colors:** Black and dark gray, high-gloss latex finish
- **Accents:** Muted, cold — the gold warmth of the original suit is gone
- **Emblem:** The bear insignia remains but rendered in black on black, visible only at certain angles
- **Cape:** Retained but darkened, heavier in appearance
- **Fit:** As skin-tight as the original, but the material reads as constraint rather than freedom
- **Eyes:** Shift to **violet** — mirroring Bill's own eye color exactly, marking Nick visibly as his

The overall impression: Supercub's silhouette, hollowed out and claimed.

### Mechanics
- Cannot be removed by Nick alone — attempts at self-removal trigger suppressive feedback from the suit
- Suppresses resistance without fully extinguishing autonomy
- Rewards compliance with low-level pleasure, punishes resistance with discomfort — the same addiction loop as the harness, scaled to full-body contact
- Redirects Nick's bearing energy outward toward Bill's targets rather than destroying it
- Requires no ongoing maintenance from Bill beyond proximity — the suit is self-reinforcing once applied

## Psychological State

Nick is **fully corrupted**.

Unlike Evan, whose corruption settled into owned equilibrium with his inner self suppressed but present, Nick's corruption goes deeper. The suit does not merely suppress him — it replaces his access to himself entirely. There is no conscious Nick watching from behind the corruption, suffering, waiting. The part of him that would resist is simply not available to him.

What Nick experiences feels real and complete. He believes he loves being at Bill's side. He receives Bill's kisses — passionate, dominating, an act of ownership made physical — as genuine affection from someone he is devoted to. He does not experience his situation as captivity. He experiences it as belonging.

Bill kisses Nick regularly, in private and in front of the Brotherhood. To Nick it feels like love. To Bill it is dominion made intimate — the most visible expression of what he has claimed. To everyone watching it is proof that Bill has achieved something unprecedented.

Nick does not perform devotion.
He believes it.
That is what makes it a corruption rather than a restraint.

---

## The Instability Tell

The corruption is genuine but not perfectly stable. Forced transformations are always less stable than consensual ones — canon law that applies even at this scale. Nick's bearing energy, relational and consent-based at its core, resists the suit at a level beneath conscious thought. Not as a will to escape, but as a physiological truth the suit cannot fully overwrite.

The visible sign: **his eyes briefly return to their natural color**.

When this happens, it is not Nick consciously surfacing. It is his bearing nature breaking through involuntarily — his body remembering what his mind has lost access to. Nick himself does not know it is happening.

In that fractional moment, Nick experiences something like waking briefly from a deep sleep. Brief confusion. Disorientation. A half-second where the certainty of his corrupted state dissolves and something older and truer surfaces without warning.

Then the suit reasserts. The violet returns. Nick is gone again — unaware anything happened.

### What Triggers It
- **Proximity to Mike.** Mike's consent-based bearing energy is the opposite frequency to everything the suit runs on. The closer Mike gets, the more frequently the flickers occur and the longer they last. Bill does not fully understand why proximity matters and works to keep them separated.
- **Direct emotional address from Mike.** Private references, shared memories, things only Nick would recognize — the suit cannot filter what it doesn't understand. Mike speaking to Nick rather than to the corruption creates fractures.
- **Accumulated proximity over time.** The flickers are not static. Each time Mike gets close, the crack widens slightly. What begins as a half-second of confusion becomes, incrementally, something longer.

### The Escalation
As Mike gets closer — physically, emotionally, persistently — the flickers grow. What starts as a brief confusion becomes a moment of recognition. What becomes recognition becomes, eventually, full surfacing.

The emotional peak of the arc: Nick breaks through completely, long enough to understand what has been done to him, long enough to see Mike, long enough to cry out for him before the suit pulls him back under.

That moment — Nick present, aware, reaching — is both the most painful beat in the story and the confirmation Mike has been fighting toward. Nick is in there. He knows what's happening in those moments. He is not beyond reach.

It is also the moment that gives Nick agency in his own rescue. He doesn't wait passively to be saved. The one time he fully surfaces, he reaches toward Mike. That is entirely consistent with who Nick is.

Bill does not know any of this is happening. He believes the suit is holding completely. Nothing in his worldview gives him a framework for what Mike and Nick are doing — reaching toward each other across a corruption he designed to be total.

That is his blind spot. And it is what undoes him.

## Nick's Function in the Brotherhood

### As Lover Boy
Nick occupies a position directly beneath Bill with no Brotherhood parallel. He is present at Bill's side in ways Boys and Sirs are not. He is displayed, shown off, treated as evidence of Bill's reach and discernment. Bill is proud of him in the way someone is proud of something rare they own.

Nick performs devotion. Bill reads the performance as real.

### As Army Engine
Nick's redirected bearing energy is Bill's force multiplier. Where Bill's own corruption methods are slow and maintenance-heavy, Nick's bearing capacity — now pointed outward under the suit's direction — can reach new recruits faster and at greater scale than Bill could manage alone.

This is the real reason Bill keeps Nick close. The lover boy status is genuine within Bill's framework, but it is also functional. Nick is both trophy and tool.

---

## The Structural Flaw

Every bearing Nick performs while actively fighting the suit from the inside is **fractured at the source**.

His resistance bleeds into the transformation. He cannot help it — his bearing energy is relational by nature, and genuine will is the mechanism. Forced to bear people against his will, the energy that reaches them carries the same instability Nick carries inside the suit.

Bill's army looks complete from the outside. Underneath, it is full of people who are still in there — suppressed, not converted. Each one is a fracture point waiting for the right conditions.

When Nick is eventually freed and the suit comes off, these corruptions begin to recalibrate. Not instantly, not dramatically — but the bearing energy already inside each recruit slowly reorients toward its original nature. Consent-based. Expansive. Self-affirming rather than hierarchical.

Bill's army doesn't collapse through rebellion.

It quietly **becomes something else** — men slowly returning to themselves inside bodies that are now genuinely, permanently their own. Not Brotherhood assets. Not Supercub's recruits either. Just people, finally allowed to arrive.

Nick heals them even while Bill is using him as a weapon. The suit redirects his energy. It cannot change what that energy fundamentally is.

---

## What Rescue Requires

Rescue is not primarily physical.

Ripping the suit off without reaching Nick first risks destabilizing him in ways that could be dangerous. The suit has been conditioning him. Removal without grounding is not guaranteed to be clean.

Mike has to reach Nick before he can free him. That means getting close enough for the eye flicker to become something more — close enough to speak to Nick directly, to address him rather than the corruption, to give Nick something to hold onto on the inside while the suit is dealt with on the outside.

The rescue is won through connection, not force.

Which is exactly what Bill cannot model, cannot predict, and cannot defend against.
