## The Brotherhood Arc — Daddy Bear's Role

### What He Has Been Doing
Since Chapter 1, Daddy Bear has been living at a cabin by a lake, northwest of the city. Old pines, a stone chimney, a splitting block outside. Removed from the city entirely. Watching.

He has no purpose left except awareness. He cannot intervene in what he sees, cannot correct the imbalances he senses, cannot reach the people whose potential he recognises. He watches Mike become Superbear from a distance. He watches the Brotherhood grow. He notices where things go underground. He carries all of it without being able to do anything with it.

This is his life. Witness without agency. Consequence without resolution.

He has been watching Bill for a long time. He knows exactly who Bill is, what he took, and what he has been building. He has watched the Brotherhood expand quietly beneath the city's notice. He knows what is being done to Nick.

He cannot act on any of it.

### Why He Hasn't Approached Mike
Daddy Bear's constraints are structural, not motivational. He does not intervene. He does not choose heroes or guide them. He places possibility into the world and steps away — that role ended with the rainbow dust.

Approaching Mike uninvited would be a form of intervention. Directing events. Choosing outcomes. The system forbids it.

He waits. Not passively — the distinction matters to him — but correctly. He will not move until someone comes to him.

### The Encounter With Mike
Mike finds Daddy Bear through the scent on the original note — a failsafe built into the paper at the time of sending. Nick's investigation surfaced the box where the note was hidden; Mike found it inside and followed the scent northwest, past the city, into open land.

Daddy Bear is chopping wood when Mike arrives. Mike de-transforms at the treeline and walks the final stretch in ordinary clothes. The old man sees him coming and is not surprised. He sets the axe into the splitting block and waits.

The conversation that follows is quiet and slightly disorienting for Mike. Daddy Bear tells him:
- Who Bill is — his real name, his origin, what he took and how
- The full history of the power that flows through Mike, Nick, and Bill
- What the corrupted suit is doing to Nick and why the corruption is unstable — and why it is structurally unstable
- That the connection between Mike and Nick was not destroyed, only overwritten, and that Nick is still underneath it

He does not give Mike a location. Instead he teaches Mike to stop reaching for Nick with panic and start listening. The direction comes from Mike himself — Daddy Bear only shows him how to be still enough to hear it. He is not a mentor in this scene. He is a source — the only person alive who holds the complete picture.

### What This Scene Means
Mike meeting Daddy Bear properly for the first time is one of the most important quiet scenes in the arc.

Mike is the most direct living consequence of what Daddy Bear set in motion. The coffee. The dust. The possibility placed into the world without command. Mike drank it, believed it, became it. Daddy Bear has been watching him from a distance since the beginning without ever being able to say so.

There is grief in this encounter that Daddy Bear does not express and Mike does not yet understand. The old man sitting across from him is the origin of everything — massive and diminished at once, carrying knowledge he cannot act on — and Mike is proof that the thing Daddy Bear sacrificed himself for actually worked.

Daddy Bear does not ask for anything.
He does not need to.
This is the only thing he has left to give — and he gives it without expectation, exactly as the system requires.

### After the Encounter
Daddy Bear returns to watching. He does not follow Mike. He does not monitor the rescue. He does not know how it ends until it is over.

When it is over — when the Brotherhood arc resolves and Nick is freed — Daddy Bear feels the shift in the city's bearing energy. He cannot see it or measure it. He simply knows, the way he knows things now: residually, without proof.

He sits with that for a while.

Then he keeps watching.

### Long Arc Significance
The Brotherhood arc is the first time Daddy Bear has been able to give something meaningful since the rainbow dust. He pointed. Someone acted. The system worked — not as he designed it, not under his guardianship, but correctly nonetheless.

Whether this constitutes the beginning of Constraint B's resolution — power freely chosen, fully embodied, offered without expectation returning to him in altered form — is left open. It is too early to say. And that uncertainty is essential.

What is certain: Mike now knows who Daddy Bear is. That knowledge changes things, even if neither of them knows yet how.
