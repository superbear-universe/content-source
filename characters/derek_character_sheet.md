# Derek — Character Sheet

## Basic Information

- **Name:** Derek
- **Age:** 46
- **Sexuality:** Gay
- **Status:** Recently out (a few months)
- **Former Life:** Married to a woman for many years; divorce finalized or in progress
- **Family Situation:** Strained relationships with family following his coming out
- **Narrative Role:** One‑chapter civilian; recipient of consent‑based bearing through Superbear

---

## Physical Appearance (Pre‑Bearing)

- **Build:** Lean but soft; not fragile, simply under‑claimed
- **Height:** Short
- **Posture:** Folded inward; shoulders slightly rounded, head often tilted down
- **Overall Impression:** A man who looks tired rather than broken — as if he has spent years minimizing himself
### Face & Hair

- **Facial Hair:** Persistent stubble he keeps hoping will finally fill in
- **Hair:** Thinning; practical cut with no styling ambition
- **Expression:** Habitually apologetic; quick to smile in self‑defense rather than confidence

### Clothing & Presentation

- **Typical Outfit:** Button‑up shirt and beige trousers — office‑coded, conservative, heterosexual‑coded
- **Visual Effect:** Respectable but anonymous; dressed for a life that no longer fits him
- **Relationship to Style:** Clothes chosen to avoid attention rather than express identity

### Facial Features & Coloring
- **Skin Tone:** Light, slightly sallow; the kind of pallor that comes from indoor routines rather than illness
- **Eyes:** Brown; warm but habitually cautious, often creased with tiredness
- **Face Shape:** Narrow, with a soft jawline that looks under-claimed rather than weak
- **Hair:** Fine and thinning, receding gently at the temples; kept neatly out of habit
- **Hands:** Practical and dry, with short nails; hands that look more used for work than for touch

---

## Personality

- **Core Traits:**
    - Self‑deprecating
    - Earnest
    - Observant
    - Patient
        
- **Social Habits:**
    - Over‑explains when nervous
    - Uses dry humor to soften moments of vulnerability
    - Apologizes pre‑emptively, often without realizing it
        

**Likeability Anchor:** Derek is quietly funny — not performing, just noticing the absurdity of himself and the world with a wry gentleness.

---

## Inner Life

Derek does not feel late to being gay — he feels late to being _alive_.

- Spent decades prioritizing stability, respectability, and other people’s comfort
- Carries grief for time lost rather than anger at the past
- Struggles with a persistent sense of unworthiness
- Asks himself “Why me?” more often than “Why not me?”

His exhaustion is not physical alone — it is the fatigue of living without embodiment.

---

## Relationship to Bear Culture

- Has been drawn to gay bear culture since his 20s
- Associates “being a bear” with:    
    - Warmth
    - Brotherhood        
    - Companionship
    - Belonging without performance
- Has never been explicitly rejected by bear spaces
- Internalizes rejection pre‑emptively, assuming he does not qualify

For Derek, the bear is not a fantasy of dominance — it is a fantasy of _being allowed to stay_.

---

## Core Wound

**Unworthiness.**

Derek does not believe he has earned joy, visibility, or transformation. When offered something good, his instinctive question is not whether he wants it — but whether he deserves it.

---

## Moment of Choice (Bearing)

- Derek’s consent is **hesitant, then firm**
- His fear is not of change, but of being unworthy of it
- He chooses bearing not to become special, but to stop disappearing

His acceptance is quiet, grounded, and resolute — a decision made for himself alone.

---
## Post‑Bearing End State
### Physical Changes

* **Overall Build:** Broad, heavy, and beary without sharp definition; strength expressed through weight and solidity rather than athletic lines
* **Neck & Shoulders:** His neck has thickened and settled into his shoulders, giving his head a grounded, anchored presence. Shoulders sit wider and lower, no longer hunched or pulled inward.
* **Posture:** Upright and open without stiffness; spine aligned naturally, occupying space without apology
* **Center of Gravity:** His weight settles lower in his body, grounding him. His stance widens instinctively, balanced and unhurried.

### Hair & Body Hair

* **Beard:** His beard has grown long, full, and dense — not styled or sculpted, just finally there, as if his face has stopped hesitating. It reads as natural completion rather than grooming ambition.
* **Chest & Arms:** Dark, natural body hair now covers his chest and arms, spreading evenly and comfortably, reinforcing warmth and bear embodiment rather than display.
* **Scalp Hair:** The thinning hair on his scalp has fallen out entirely. His head is now cleanly bald, a change that feels neither loss nor shock — simply final, as if uncertainty has let go.

### Face & Expression

* **Facial Structure:** Cheeks are fuller, jaw heavier and more settled, face integrated naturally with his larger body
* **Expression:** Quieter and calmer; the habitual apologetic reflex is gone. His eyes meet the world at level rather than upward.

### Hands & Stillness

* **Hands:** Where his hands once fidgeted or folded inward, they now rest easily at his sides. Movements are deliberate, stillness comfortable.

### Presence & Emotional State

* **Presence:** Renewed energy paired with calm stillness; he feels present in his body rather than observing it from a distance
* **Emotional Tone:** Belonging

Derek does not feel transformed into someone else.

He feels like the man he has always been has finally been allowed to arrive.

---

## Core Themes

- Late self‑actualization
- Embodiment as belonging
- Masculinity without dominance
- Desire without entitlement
- Presence as healing

---

_Derek exists for a single chapter. His story is complete the moment he no longer feels the need to apologize for taking up space._
