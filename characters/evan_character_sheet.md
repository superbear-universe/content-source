# Evan — Character Sheet

**Status:** Brotherhood Enforcer (Active)  
**Bearing Type:** Corrupted Bearing (Non-consensual / Ownership-Based)  
**Affiliation:** Butch Bear’s Brotherhood  
**Narrative Role:** Corrupted friend; potential fracture point  
**First Appearance:** Chapter 5  
**Reappearance (Post-Bearing):** Chapter 7

---

## Overview

Evan is a close friend of Nick who is forcibly inducted into the Brotherhood on the same night as the concert in Chapter 5. His transformation occurs rapidly, without meaningful opportunity for informed consent, and results in a state of controlled stability enforced through a permanent harness.

Evan represents **how quickly agency can be stripped**, how corruption can look calm and functional, and why rescue is not always welcomed by the person who needs it most.

---

## Pre-Bearing Evan (Chapter 5 — Earlier That Evening)

### Appearance (Visual Canon)

- **Build:** Fit, athletic, naturally sturdy; not bulky or exaggerated  
- **Height:** Average to slightly above average  
- **Posture:** Relaxed, open, socially at ease  
- **Body Hair:** Light to moderate  
- **Facial Hair:** Light stubble or clean-shaven  
- **Hair:** Short, dark, casually styled  
- **Clothing:** Black t-shirt, dark jeans, casual sneakers  

**Overall Impression:**  
Approachable. Grounded. The kind of guy who looks comfortable in his own skin without trying to dominate a space.

---

### Personality (Pre-Bearing)

- Calm, steady presence
- Socially adaptable
- Not dominant, not passive
- Protective without being patronizing
- Emotionally reliable

Evan is not a jock and not a wallflower.  
He moves easily between social groups and never feels out of place.

---

### Social World

- **Gym World:** Casual lifter; trains regularly but not obsessively  
- **Friends:** Includes both gym-adjacent men and gentler, nerdy types (Nick)  
- **Subculture:** Metal / alt scene  
- **Music Taste:** Doom and sludge metal, including bands like *Black Ursa*  

Evan enjoys intensity — loud music, physicality, shared experience — but not hierarchy.

---

### Relationship to Nick (Pre-Bearing)

- Longstanding friendship
- Nick trusts Evan implicitly
- Evan often functions as the “steady one”
- Comfortable emotional baseline

Nick never questions Evan’s presence or intentions.

This trust is the opening that allows Evan to be taken.

---

## The Recruitment (Canon Event)

**Timeline:**  
Occurs **between** Evan getting drinks with Nick in Chapter 5 and Nick waking restrained in Chapter 7 — all within the same concert night.

### Key Facts

- Evan does **not** seek out the Brotherhood
- He does **not** knowingly consent to bearing
- He is likely:
  - separated from Nick briefly
  - isolated
  - pressured or restrained
- The harness is placed on him **before full comprehension**

By Butch Bear’s standards, this is acceptable.

Consent is not required.  
Compliance is sufficient.

The harness is **always worn**.

It is not symbolic.  
It is a **conduit**.

### Functions

- Stabilizes Butch Bear’s control
- Narrows Evan’s internal decision space
- Enforces obedience and discipline
- Rewards compliance with **sexualized pleasure**

## Psychological State

Evan exists in **owned equilibrium**.

- Calm
- Functional
- Disciplined
- Not cruel
- Not joyful

He does not enjoy hurting others.  
He simply performs his role.

This makes him more dangerous than someone overtly violent.

---

## Recognition & Suppression

### Default State

- Evan does **not** recognize Nick as “Nick”
- Nick is processed as:
  - an asset
  - a variable
  - a risk

### Buried State (Canonical)

- Evan still knows Nick
- The memory is suppressed, not erased
- It can resurface if Evan is:
  - isolated
  - spoken to quietly
  - addressed emotionally rather than confrontationally

When recognition surfaces:
- his expression softens
- his breath hitches
- hesitation appears

The harness responds immediately with pleasure to suppress it.

Saving Evan means fighting **addiction**, not just control.

---

## Relationship to Butch Bear

Evan is not a favorite.

He is useful.

Butch Bear:
- trusts him
- deploys him
- assumes loyalty is permanent

This assumption is incorrect.

---

## Narrative Function

Evan exists to show:

- how fast corruption can happen
- how little consent Butch Bear needs
- how loss of agency can look calm and stable
- that friendship does not grant immunity

He is the personal cost of the Brotherhood.

---

## Fracture Potential

Evan is a **viable fracture point**.

Not because he rebels—
but because he remembers.

Memory and ownership cannot coexist indefinitely.

If Evan breaks, it will be:
- quiet
- procedural
- subtle

Just enough.

---

## Core Theme

Evan answers the question:

> *What if it happened to someone you loved — fast, efficiently, and without drama?*

He is not a villain.

He is the proof that rescue is not simple, and freedom is not always immediately desirable once control has been internalized.
