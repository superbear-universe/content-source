# Jacob — Character Sheet

## Basic Information
- **Name:** Jacob
- **Age:** Mid-twenties
- **Sexuality:** Gay
- **Location:** Mid-sized city, far side of the country from the main city
- **Life Status:** Financially managing, emotionally stuck
- **Bearing Type:** Normal Bearing (accidental, consensual)
- **Narrative Role:** Nick's first accidental bearing; standalone transformation story

---

## Appearance (Before Transformation)

Jacob is **overweight without being beary**. His body carries mass without presence — soft, unmuscled, and largely hairless in a way that reads as unresolved rather than chosen. He does not look like a bear waiting to happen. He looks like someone the world has not yet asked anything of.

- **Build:** Overweight, soft-framed; carries significant mass across his stomach, chest, and face, but without the density or hair that would read as bear-adjacent. His weight sits on him rather than in him.
- **Height:** Average to slightly below average
- **Posture:** Rounded inward, apologetic. He takes up more physical space than his posture suggests he believes he's entitled to — a constant, low-level contradiction he doesn't examine.
- **Hair:** Ginger — a warm, reddish-orange that is immediately distinctive. Kept short and unremarkably styled, as if he hasn't decided what to do with it yet.
- **Eyes:** Green — a clear, warm green that is the most striking thing about his face, though Jacob himself has never quite registered this. Slightly downcast in social situations, rarely used to hold attention deliberately.
- **Body Hair:** Minimal. Bare chest, smooth arms, little visible hair anywhere. Nothing that signals the body he is about to become.
- **Facial Hair:** Clean-shaven. Not by strong preference — simply because he has never thought of himself as someone a beard would suit.
- **Face:** Round, soft-featured, with a warm and slightly uncertain expression. His face is not unattractive — it is unasserted. He smiles easily but rarely fully.
- **Skin Tone:** Fair, with the light freckling that often accompanies ginger colouring. Flushes easily.
- **Clothing:**
  - Oversized t-shirts and hoodies worn loose — not for style, but to minimize
  - Dark jeans, practical and plain
  - Clean sneakers
  - He dresses to disappear, choosing clothes that contain him rather than present him

**Overall Impression:**
Jacob looks like someone apologizing for being in the room. His body is present and impossible to ignore in the purely physical sense — but he carries it in a way that asks you not to look too closely. There is nothing wrong with him. He has simply never been given permission to arrive.

---

## Personality

- Warm and quietly funny in private, notably more subdued in public
- Intellectually engaged — sharp when comfortable, retreating when not
- Avoidant of situations that draw attention to his body
- Loyal to the point of self-erasure with people he genuinely cares about
- Perceptive about others; less so about himself
- Carries self-consciousness as background noise rather than acute pain — it is so constant he has largely stopped noticing it

Jacob is not depressed. He is not broken. He simply moves through the world at a reduced volume, and has for long enough that he no longer remembers choosing to.

---

## Inner Life

Jacob's relationship with his body is one of **resigned management**.

He is overweight. He has tried not to be. Diets attempted and abandoned, a gym phase that lasted three months, the usual arithmetic of effort and outcome that never resolved the way it was supposed to. He does not hate himself for any of this. He has simply arrived at a quiet, settled conclusion: this is the body he has, and the body he has is not the kind that gets to take up space confidently.

What he wants — vaguely, without examining it too closely — is to feel comfortable. Not thin. Not transformed. Just comfortable. He does not have a clear image of what that would look like, because every version of it he can imagine involves being smaller.

He has never seriously considered that it might involve being **more**.

---

## The Crush on Ben

Jacob and Ben have been living together for over a year.

Ben is openly gay — but Jacob didn't know this when they moved in together, and by the time he worked it out, he had already convinced himself it was irrelevant. Because Ben is attractive and comfortable in himself and socially at ease in all the ways Jacob is not, and Jacob has had enough experience of that particular arithmetic to know how it usually resolves.

So he carries the crush quietly. Does not name it, does not act on it, does not allow himself to think about it for very long at any given time. He has become practised at this.

What Jacob does not know: Ben has been carrying his own feelings about Jacob since approximately the third week they lived together, and has said nothing for reasons that are structurally identical to Jacob's own silence. Neither of them knows the other is stuck in the same place.

---

## Relationship to Nick

Nick and Jacob met through a shared online D&D campaign several years ago. They have never met in person — the distance between their cities was always too significant, the logistics never quite working out, the means never quite there.

What they have instead is **years of genuine friendship** conducted entirely through screens and voice chat. Nick knows Jacob's humor, his anxieties, his tendency to deflect difficult feelings with self-deprecating jokes. Jacob knows Nick as a warm, thoughtful presence who listens properly and doesn't push.

Jacob has seen Nick on webcam — face, voice, the familiar frame of whoever he was before. He has no full picture of what Nick looks like now.

When Nick arrives at his door for the first time, the gap between expectation and reality is significant.

---

## The Bearing — How It Happens

Nick arrives. Jacob opens the door.

The difference between the Nick he knew from a screen and the Nick standing in front of him is immediately disorienting — not wrong, but vivid. Broad, solid, bearded, completely at ease in a body that seems to know exactly what it is. Jacob doesn't say anything about it immediately. He lets Nick in, makes coffee, and they fall into the easy rhythm of a friendship that doesn't require warmup.

But the image of Nick — comfortable, grounded, inhabiting himself so completely — has cracked something open without Jacob noticing.

It surfaces during a quiet moment. Not dramatically. Jacob makes a self-deprecating joke about his body — the kind he has made a thousand times, the kind that deflects before anyone else can comment. Nick doesn't laugh. He doesn't offer reassurance either. He just — waits, the way he does.

And Jacob, in the specific safety of someone who has known him for years and is sitting right there and is not going anywhere, says the thing he has never quite said out loud before.

Not a dramatic confession. Just the quiet, tired truth of what it has been like to live in this body and believe, consistently, that it is a problem to be managed.

Nick recognises it. He recognises it from the inside.

He pulls Jacob into a hug — not performing comfort, just offering it. Genuine, grounded contact between two people who trust each other completely.

And something moves.

Nick doesn't understand what's happening until it has already begun. His bearing energy, responding to genuine emotional connection and physical contact, crosses the threshold without his permission. He feels it leave him — a warmth, a transfer — and pulls back immediately, alarmed.

Too late.

---

## The Transformation

Jacob's transformation is **not what he would have chosen**.

His first response is alarm — the heat spreading under his skin, the unfamiliar pressure of his body beginning to change, the immediate panicked thought that something is wrong with him. He has spent his adult life wishing his body were different. But not like this. Not without understanding what is happening.

For a long moment he resists it, or tries to. His body does not stop.

Then something shifts.

The warmth stops feeling like intrusion and starts feeling like **recognition**. The pressure beneath his skin stops being frightening and starts being — right. His body is not becoming something foreign. It is becoming something that was structurally true about him all along, something he could never have imagined because every version of himself he had access to involved being less.

He is becoming **more**.

The transformation is full and unhurried:
- His frame widens and deepens, mass redistributing into dense, substantial muscle beneath softness that does not disappear
- His chest and stomach fill out with power — the belly remains, but it is grounded now, belonging to a body that knows what it is
- Body hair arrives steadily across his chest, stomach, arms — thick and distinctly ginger-auburn, unmistakably beary
- His face changes last: jaw squaring slightly, features settling into something more defined and assured
- The beard comes in dense and full — a rich auburn-ginger with the first suggestion of copper highlights — and transforms his face completely

When it is over, Jacob stands in the middle of his apartment looking at his hands.

Then he looks up.

The mirror in the hallway catches him, and he goes still.

The horror has passed. What replaces it is something he has no prepared word for — a warmth behind his sternum, a quiet, private joy at the simple fact of existing inside this body. He does not look like the version of himself he spent years trying to diet his way toward. He looks like something else entirely.

He looks, for the first time, like himself.

---

## Appearance (After Transformation)

Jacob is **a large, proud chubby muscle bear** — and he knows it.

- **Build:** Broad, thick, and powerful. Dense muscle layered under and alongside significant softness — a body that is both strong and substantial, occupying space with complete authority. His belly is present and grounded, part of the architecture rather than an apology.
- **Hair:** The same ginger, now worn differently — the same man, but one who has stopped apologizing for the colour.
- **Eyes:** The same green — but where they were once downcast and uncertain, they now hold contact easily. The warmth was always there. Now it has somewhere to land.
- **Posture:** Open, upright, expansive. He takes up the space he was always physically occupying but never believed he was allowed to.
- **Body Hair:** Dense and thick across chest, stomach, and arms — a rich ginger-auburn. Unmistakably beary.
- **Facial Hair:** A full, magnificent beard — dense auburn-ginger with copper highlights, deepening toward the edges. Frames his face completely. Transforms it.
- **Skin:** The same fair complexion and light freckles, now worn without self-consciousness.
- **Clothing:** Nothing he owns fits anymore.

**Overall Impression:**
Jacob looks like a man who has arrived. Not changed — arrived. His body is not a different body. It is his body, finally given permission to be completely itself.

---

## Ben's Reaction

Ben comes home to find Jacob transformed and Nick looking slightly stunned in the kitchen.

The conversation that follows is not the one Jacob expected to have.

Ben, who has been quietly carrying feelings for Jacob for over a year, does not fall for the new body. He fell for Jacob a long time ago. What he says, when he finally says it, is that he always had — and that the reason he never said anything was not Jacob's body but his own uncertainty about how Jacob felt.

Jacob, who spent a year convinced the situation was impossible, has to absorb two things simultaneously: that he was wrong about his body not being worth loving, and that he was wrong about the whole situation being unwinnable from the start.

He takes a moment.

Then he laughs — properly, freely, the laugh of someone who has just discovered they were wrong about something in the best possible way.

## Core Themes

- Embodiment as revelation, not correction
- The body you have versus the body you're allowed to want
- Friendship as the safest place for truth
- Being seen before being changed
- Arrival rather than transformation

---

## Narrative Role

Jacob exists to demonstrate that **bearing does not fix people — it reveals them**.

He was not broken before. He was not wrong. He was carrying a version of himself shaped by years of being told his body was a problem, and bearing dissolved that architecture and showed him what had been underneath it the whole time.

He does not become loveable after the transformation.
He was already loved.
He just couldn't see it yet.
