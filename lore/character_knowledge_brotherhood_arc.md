# Character Knowledge Document — The Brotherhood Arc

## Overview

This document tracks what Mike and Nick know about Butch Bear and the Brotherhood at each stage of the arc, and how that knowledge shapes their decisions. It exists to maintain consistency across chapters and prevent characters from acting on information they don’t yet have.

-----

## Before the Arc — Shared Knowledge (Post Chapter 7)

After Nick’s rescue in Chapter 7, Mike and Nick have had ongoing conversations about what happened and why. Neither has the full picture, but they are not starting from zero.

### What Both Know

- Someone organised and deliberate took Nick in Chapter 7
- The operation was large enough to have a dedicated location and multiple men
- Evan is inside the Brotherhood, corrupted, wearing a permanent harness
- Whoever runs it has significant power — enough to have transformed Evan completely
- The Brotherhood has a presence in the city that predates Nick’s capture

### What Neither Knows

- Bill’s real name or civilian identity
- His origin or connection to Daddy Bear
- The full scale of the Brotherhood’s operation
- That Bill has superhero-level bearing powers
- The army plan or its ultimate goal
- That Daddy Bear is still alive and watching the city

-----

## The Investigation — How Knowledge Develops

### Nick’s Role

Nick leads the intellectual work. He was in that room in Chapter 7 longer than Mike, under different conditions, and came away with more raw material — he heard Bill speak, observed him directly, saw how the Brotherhood operated up close. His analytical disposition means he has been quietly building a picture ever since.

Nick brings findings to Mike as he develops them. He wants to work on this as a team.

### Mike’s Role

Mike is aware of the Brotherhood as a threat and genuinely wants it stopped. But his papa bear instinct keeps reaching for control when he’s scared. Every time Nick brings new findings, Mike’s response is to pump the brakes — it’s too dangerous, they need more information, they should wait. He frames this as protecting Nick. Underneath it is a condescension he doesn’t fully recognise in himself: Nick is newer to this, less experienced, and Mike keeps treating that as a reason to shut him out rather than work alongside him.

This is a violation of their relationship’s core rules — making unilateral decisions about Nick’s involvement, minimising his agency under the guise of protection — but Mike doesn’t frame it that way. He frames it as love.

### The Pattern

Nick brings findings. Mike pushes back. Nick brings more findings. Mike pushes back again. Eventually Nick stops sharing everything in real time because Mike’s response is always the same. The investigation doesn’t go underground out of deception. It goes underground out of frustration.

This is the quiet breach that precedes the larger one.

-----

## What Nick Knows at the Point of Going Alone

By the time Nick locates Evan, his investigation has established:

- The Brotherhood’s name and approximate structure — Boys, Sirs, a central authority
- That the operation is larger than a single location
- That the man who runs it has bearing-based superpowers comparable to Mike’s
- Evan’s approximate location within Brotherhood territory
- Enough about Brotherhood movement patterns to find where Evan is being held

What Nick still doesn’t know:

- Bill’s name, identity, or origin
- The Daddy Bear connection
- The army plan
- The underground location where he will eventually be held

### The Breach

Nick finds Evan’s location and tells Mike. Mike shuts it down again — same response, same pattern. Nick, whose frustration has been building for the entire investigation, goes alone regardless.

This is not a foolish decision from Nick’s perspective. It is a stubborn one made in response to a genuine grievance. He is smarter than Mike and knows it. He is tired of being managed.

He is also wrong to go alone. Both things are true.

-----

## What Mike Knows at the Point of Nick’s Capture

When Nick doesn’t come back, Mike knows:

- Where Nick went — the location Nick told him about
- That Nick isn’t there anymore — the trail goes cold somewhere in the city
- That whoever has Nick has moved him somewhere Mike cannot track him to
- The contents of Nick’s investigation notes, whatever he left behind

What Mike doesn’t know:

- Where Nick is being held
- That the location is underground, shielding Nick’s scent and presence
- Bill’s identity, powers, or origin
- The army plan now underway beneath the city

### Mike’s Starting Point

Nick’s notes are the beginning of the investigation, not the end. They give Mike a direction but not a destination. The Brotherhood location Nick found is abandoned or locked down by the time Mike gets there. The underground lair is genuinely unfindable through tracking alone — distance, concrete, unfamiliar urban territory breaks the trail before it leads anywhere useful.

Mike is stuck. He has never been stuck like this before.

-----

## The Gap — What Mike Cannot Find Alone

Mike’s super smell can track Nick across open territory but loses the trail somewhere in the city. The underground location is shielded by geography — stone, concrete, depth — not by any deliberate trick. There are physical limits to any sense, even a superhuman one.

Mike knows Nick is somewhere in the city. He cannot pinpoint where. He investigates through Brotherhood patterns, known locations, anything Nick’s notes point toward. None of it leads to the underground lair.

This is the investigation arc’s central obstacle. Mike cannot fight his way to Nick because he cannot find Nick. His strength is useless without a direction.

-----

## Daddy Bear — The Missing Piece

Daddy Bear has been watching the city’s edges for a long time. He notices patterns nobody else notices because nobody else is looking at the city the way he is — without purpose, without destination, with nothing but time and residual awareness.

He has watched the Brotherhood grow. He has noticed where things go underground. He cannot intervene, cannot fight, cannot restore himself or direct events. But he can witness.

When Mike finds him — an old man still massive in frame, diminished by age and loss rather than illness — it is not a dramatic encounter. It is quiet and slightly disorienting for Mike, who doesn’t immediately understand what he’s looking at.

### What Daddy Bear Knows

- Who Bill is — his real name, his origin, what he took and how
- The full history of the power that flows through Mike, Nick, and Bill
- That the connection between Mike and Nick was overwritten, not destroyed, and that Nick is still underneath it
- What the corruption is doing to Nick and why it is structurally unstable

### What Daddy Bear Cannot Do

- Intervene directly
- Fight
- Accompany Mike
- Guarantee the outcome

He can only point. That is the one thing his constraints allow, and it is what he does.

### The Encounter

Mike and Daddy Bear meeting properly for the first time is one of the quieter and more important scenes in the arc. Mike is Superbear — the most direct living consequence of what Daddy Bear set in motion. Daddy Bear has been watching him from a distance since the beginning.

The conversation happens at Daddy Bear’s cabin, northwest of the city. Daddy Bear tells Mike who Bill is, where the power comes from, and what the corruption has done to Nick. He does not give Mike a location. Instead he teaches Mike to stop forcing the connection and start listening. The direction comes from Mike himself — Daddy Bear only creates the conditions for him to find it.

Daddy Bear does not ask for anything in return.
He does not need to.
This is the only thing he has left to give.

-----

## What Mike Knows Before the Rescue

After the Daddy Bear encounter, Mike has the full picture for the first time:

- A direction to Nick — found through the connection itself, not given by Daddy Bear
- Who Bill is and what his powers are
- The Daddy Bear connection and the origin of all bearing power
- The army plan and its scale
- What the corrupted suit is doing to Nick and why it is unstable

He goes in knowing exactly what he’s facing.

-----

## The Aftermath — What Needs to Be Addressed

When Nick is rescued, the relationship has two unresolved breaches that need to be faced:

**Mike’s breach:** Repeated protectiveness that crossed into control, shutting Nick out of a shared investigation, making unilateral decisions about Nick’s involvement under the guise of love. This is what created the conditions for Nick going alone.

**Nick’s breach:** Going alone regardless, making a unilateral safety decision that affected both of them, exactly what their relationship rules identify as unacceptable.

Neither is solely responsible for what happened. Both have something to reckon with.

Their relationship document establishes that they realign through honest conversation and quiet physical closeness. That conversation — after the rescue, after Nick has had time to return to himself — is where the arc emotionally resolves.
