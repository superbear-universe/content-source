# AFTER CORRUPTION
## Andrew — The Sir

---

## What Changed

Andrew is not a different person.

He is the same intelligence, the same perceptiveness, the same hunger for arrival — now housed in a body that commands attention and a role that demands it.

The boy who wanted to be taken seriously finally is.

That is the cruelty of it.

---

## Appearance (Post-Corruption)

Andrew no longer looks 20. He looks **experienced** — rugged, settled, and physically dominant in a way that reads as earned rather than given.

* **Build:** Massively muscular, thick through chest, shoulders, and arms; powerful legs; a solid, heavy gut that adds authority rather than softness
* **Height:** Same 1.84m, but now carried with complete confidence — he fills vertical space rather than apologizing for it
* **Posture:** Open, expansive, deliberate; he takes up exactly as much space as he wants
* **Body Hair:** Dense and dark across chest, stomach, and arms; a visible contrast to the smooth skin he once had
* **Facial Hair:** Full, long beard — closer in length to Butch Bear's than to the Boys' shorter beards; deliberately maintained, a visible marker of rank
* **Face:** The youthful softness is gone; replaced by harder lines, a more settled jaw, an expression that reads as authority rather than uncertainty
* **Eyes:** Same brown — but now steady, assessing, unreadable behind aviators when in role
* **Voice:** Deep, measured, unhurried; he does not raise it

**Overall Impression:**
Andrew looks like a man who has been somewhere and come back changed. Not young. Not soft. Rugged in the way that suggests experience rather than age — which is precisely the effect. He looks older than he is, and he knows it, and he uses it.

---

## Attire (Post-Corruption)

Andrew's attire reflects his rank within the Brotherhood — distinct from both the Boys beneath him and the Alpha above him.

* **Shirt:** Tight-fitting black leather shirt; short-sleeved, skin-tight, emphasizing arms and chest without obscuring them
* **Bottoms:** Black leather pants, form-fitting
* **Boots:** Heavy black lace-up leather boots
* **Cap:** Black leather cap, worn with authority
* **Sunglasses:** Aviator sunglasses — worn in the field, removed only when he chooses to make direct eye contact a deliberate act
* **No harness**

The absence of a harness is the most significant detail. Boys wear harnesses because they require mechanical enforcement. Andrew does not. His loyalty has been internalized — not because it was freely given, but because Butch Bear's hold no longer needs hardware to hold.

---

## Personality (Post-Corruption)

Andrew is not cruel. He is **efficient**.

* Commands without raising his voice
* Expects obedience and receives it without drama
* Shows no visible emotion in the field
* Treats Boys as assets, not people — not with contempt, simply without sentiment
* Reserved around the Alpha; attentive, deliberate, unfailingly loyal

He does not perform dominance. He simply inhabits it, the way he once wished he could inhabit his own body.

---

## Role Within the Brotherhood

Andrew functions as **commander in the Alpha's absence**.

* Manages the Boys operationally
* Makes tactical decisions independently
* Enforces Brotherhood ideology through authority rather than force
* Interfaces with the Alpha as a trusted subordinate, not a soldier

He is the layer between Butch Bear's absolute authority and the Boys' absolute obedience. He thinks, plans, and leads. That capacity is precisely why he was selected.

---

## Pleasure Mechanics (Post-Corruption)

Andrew's corruption is maintained not by hardware but by **bidirectional reward**.

**Downward pleasure:**
Andrew receives genuine, sexualized satisfaction from commanding the Boys. Giving an order and watching it obeyed. Correcting a Boy's posture. The steady, reinforcing warmth of authority exercised without resistance.

**Upward pleasure:**
Andrew receives a separate, distinct reward from serving Butch Bear directly. Being acknowledged by the Alpha. Acting in his interest. The quiet, deep satisfaction of being trusted at the highest level of the hierarchy.

These two streams do not compete. They **reinforce** each other — every act of command reminds him why the Alpha's authority is worth serving; every act of service reminds him of the rank he holds over everyone else.

This is what makes Sirs more stable than Boys. They are not maintained by external enforcement. They are maintained by **genuine satisfaction flowing in both directions**.

---

## The Buried Self

Andrew retains a buried self — but it is harder to reach than Evan's.

**Why it's different from Evan:**
Evan's buried self surfaces through *emotional memory*. Call his name, reference something he cared about, and something flickers. His suppression is mechanical; the self underneath it is intact.

Andrew's buried self surfaces through *intelligence*. Somewhere beneath the satisfaction and the certainty and the genuine pleasure of his role, a part of him is still smart enough to notice the architecture of his own cage. Not to grieve what he's lost — but to *perceive* it. To understand, at some cold and distant level, that he is being used. That his desires were not satisfied so much as they were **redirected**.

He does not act on this perception. The satisfaction is too immediate, too real, too constant. Why leave a cage when the cage feels like exactly where you're supposed to be?

But the perception exists.

And it cannot be fully extinguished.

---

## Rescue Difficulty

Andrew is the hardest rescue in the Brotherhood — not because his suppression is deepest, but because his desires were **genuinely met**.

To rescue Evan, you appeal to what was taken.
To rescue Andrew, you have to convince him that what he has is not what he actually wanted.

That is a much harder argument to make to someone who has exactly what they spent their whole life waiting for.

## Core Themes

* Intelligence as both weapon and vulnerability
* Arrival at the wrong destination
* The tragedy of genuine fulfillment
* Authority without freedom
* Perception without liberation
