Nick surfaced slowly.

Not all at once — more like drifting upward through layers of sensation. Weight first. Then softness. Then the dull, persistent ache behind his eyes that told him he hadn’t imagined any of it.

A bed.

His bed.

The familiar mattress dipped beneath his shoulders, the faint scent of detergent he always forgot the name of. Home. The realization settled something in his chest he hadn’t realized was clenched.

Nick breathed out, long and careful.

Someone was with him.

The presence wasn’t looming. It didn’t press. It simply *was* — steady, anchored, close enough to feel without touching.

When Nick opened his eyes, the room was dim, lit by the spill of streetlight through the curtains. A large figure sat beside the bed, framed in shadow and unmistakable even before his vision fully sharpened.

Superbear.

He was seated in the chair Nick usually kicked clothes over — an ordinary thing, light wood and straight-backed, pulled close to the mattress. It was clearly not made for someone his size. He didn’t sit back in it so much as perch, knees drawn up higher than comfortable, shoulders rounded forward. His forearms rested on his thighs, hands loosely clasped between them, fingers flexing now and then without purpose.

The cape was gone. Folded somewhere out of sight. Without it, the suit felt quieter, its colors dulled by the low light, stretched over a body that looked heavy in a way Nick hadn’t seen before.

Waiting.

Nick swallowed. His throat felt dry.

“Hey,” he rasped.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/e4ce277502649d2419007821b4a4a34e/ad5fa3e4cd08410d-8f/s640x960/b79df9cccc8a69408fa00f16b0615b939db4ef1d.png)

Superbear’s head snapped up immediately.

“You’re awake,” he said, relief rushing into his voice so fast it nearly tripped the words. “Easy. Don’t sit up yet. You hit your head pretty hard.”

Nick let himself sink back into the pillow. “Yeah,” he murmured. “Feels like it.”

Superbear leaned forward instinctively, then stopped himself, settling back into the chair instead. One hand lifted, hovered, then came to rest on the edge of the seat as if he were reminding himself where his weight belonged.

“You’re safe,” he said quietly. “I’ve got you.”

The words landed strange — familiar in a way Nick couldn’t immediately place.

Nick’s gaze drifted to Superbear’s face.

From this distance, there was nowhere for performance to hide.

The beard was the same shape he remembered, fuller than most people wore it, softer at the edges than it looked on the news. His mouth was pulled tight, like he was holding something back. And his eyes —

Nick frowned faintly.

They weren’t scanning the room. They weren’t sharp or vigilant or heroic.

They were fixed on Nick, open and raw, like nothing else existed.

“You’re shaking,” Nick said softly.

Superbear inhaled.

Held it.

Let it out again, uneven.

“I thought…” His voice faltered. “I thought I’d lost you.”

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/243294b88658e3ccd61d2591bb8836d6/ad5fa3e4cd08410d-17/s640x960/dec00d1b6fd10cc205967a6586463e6664a8ffc7.png)

The words cracked something wide open.

Superbear turned his face away, staring down at the floor between his boots like he could still pull himself back together if he just tried hard enough. His shoulders hunched further, pressing inward as if he were trying to take up less space in a body that refused to cooperate.

It didn’t work.

The sound that broke out of him was small and wrecked, dragged up from somewhere he’d been holding shut for far too long. His breath hitched, once, twice — then the tears came, hot and unstoppable.

“I should’ve been there sooner,” he choked. “I knew this could happen. I *knew*—”

His hands clenched together, knuckles whitening where they rested between his knees.

“I thought if I stayed careful,” he went on, words tumbling now, “if I kept my distance, if I didn’t let you get pulled into any of this—”

His voice broke completely.

“I was so scared.”

Superbear dragged in a breath that didn’t seem to go anywhere. He scrubbed a hand over his face, fingers catching in his beard, then let his arm drop again like it was too heavy to hold up.

“I told myself it was about responsibility,” he said hoarsely. “About not pulling you into something you didn’t ask for.”

He shook his head, breath stuttering like it couldn’t decide what to do next.

“But that wasn’t the whole truth.”

His gaze stayed fixed on the floor between his boots.

“I was scared,” he said quietly. “Because somewhere along the way, I think I—”

The words caught. He swallowed hard.

“I think I love you.”

Nick watched him come apart.

Not the hero.

Not the symbol.

Just a man in a suit, folded forward in a chair too small for him, crying like he’d been bracing himself against disaster and finally realized it had already passed.

Something inside Nick clicked into place.

Not a thought.  
Not a theory.

Recognition.

Nick let out a shaky breath, something between a laugh and a sigh.

“Mike?”

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/da5a1e374d41d2c9d9e423c7417c93d3/ad5fa3e4cd08410d-cc/s640x960/e114863920af834d8989391361e8c7c31aa188e3.png)

The name didn’t cut through the sobbing so much as still it.

Superbear froze.

For a second, he didn’t look like he could speak at all. His breath caught halfway in, shoulders going rigid as if the sound of his name had reached someplace he hadn’t meant to open. Slowly, he lifted his head.

His eyes were red. Bare. Unguarded.

There was no confusion in them. No denial.

Just the quiet devastation of being seen.

“—yeah,” he whispered, the word breaking apart as it left him.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/5f2725506425fcb8ed8c3fce1a51e2a5/ad5fa3e4cd08410d-66/s640x960/debceae267cdaa7b81f1a8b7a6908a8e23df2a1d.png)

That was all it took.

Nick swallowed, his chest tight in a way that hurt differently than his head did. He didn’t feel shocked. He didn’t feel afraid.

He felt… sad. And relieved. And unbearably tender all at once.

It made sense now. Too much sense.

“I didn’t want you to find out like this,” Mike said softly.

Nick shifted carefully, wincing as his temples protested, and reached out from the bed. His fingers brushed Mike’s forearm — solid, warm, unmistakably real.

“It’s okay,” Nick said quietly. “I’m okay.”

Mike’s shoulders sagged at that, like the last thing holding him upright had finally given way.

The suit didn’t vanish in a flash.

It loosened.

Color dimmed. The weight seemed to ebb, like it was no longer needed here. Light folded in on itself quietly, leaving behind familiar clothes, familiar lines.

Mike sat there instead — glasses slightly crooked, shoulders still hunched, tears clinging stubbornly to his lashes.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/01ab91fc8e961068265de0db55bc363a/ad5fa3e4cd08410d-d7/s640x960/f077c3bec7bd8d382d6ed53b8f81ba8ed7e5eb17.png)

The same man.

Nick’s hand stayed where it was.

Neither of them spoke for a long moment.

Outside, the city kept moving.

Inside, everything had finally stopped.

# **- - -**

Morning came quietly.

Nick woke with the dull ache of sleep that hadn’t been deep enough — his body stiff and heavy in a way that felt earned instead of alarming. He lay still for a moment, staring at the ceiling, aware without being disoriented, letting the last of the night settle.

Then he turned his head toward the chair near the bed.

It was empty now.

The blanket that had been draped over it was folded neatly over the back, set there with intention instead of fatigue. Mike hadn’t slept much — if at all. He’d kept watch, and then he’d moved on.

Nick exhaled slowly.

From somewhere beyond the bedroom came soft movement. A cupboard opening, then closing again. The faint clink of ceramic set down with more care than necessary. Sounds made by someone who was awake, careful, already elsewhere.

So Mike hadn’t left.

But he also hadn’t stayed.

The memory of last night pressed in at the edges — the fear, the guilt, the way Mike had folded inward as soon as the danger passed. Care, for him, didn’t always look like closeness — not when he was afraid. It looked like vigilance first. Distance second.

Nick pushed the covers aside and sat up. His feet touched the floor, grounding him. This was still his apartment. Still his morning. Still his choice.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/2152be8ebec24ca874576eb30eb4401d/ad5fa3e4cd08410d-54/s640x960/a86300e0f69c455c6298f4d808004a0c4a915e85.png)

The quiet didn’t feel fragile. It felt provisional — like something waiting to be decided.

He stood, pulled on a shirt, and followed the sound of movement toward the kitchen.

Not drifting.  
Not waiting.

Choosing.

The kitchen smelled faintly of coffee.

Mike stood beside the counter, sleeves pushed up, his forearms resting against the edge like he needed the contact. They were big, solid with quiet strength — and now Nick knew what they were capable of. His hands were spread wide and careful, as if he was constantly measuring how much pressure the world could take from him.

The kettle sat in front of him, filled but untouched.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/287cf90bf202ce78ce7cc9acda80ad56/ad5fa3e4cd08410d-cc/s640x960/37eea0d5b497f53529b74a1b23bba14e862c0e1a.png)

Nick paused in the doorway.

He hadn’t announced himself. Hadn’t needed to. Mike’s shoulders shifted anyway, the movement subtle but immediate, like his body had registered Nick before his mind caught up. When he turned, his hands lifted from the counter at once, fingers flexing briefly before settling again at his sides.

“Morning,” Mike said.

Not careful. Not overly bright. Just there.

“Morning,” Nick replied.

He crossed the kitchen at his own pace. His body still ached in that dull, persistent way that only made itself known once everything else had quieted down. He pulled out a chair and sat, feeling the solidity of it beneath him, grounding.

For a moment, neither of them spoke.

Mike reached for the kettle and set it on the stove, movements unhurried. The soft click of the switch felt louder than it should have.

“I didn’t know what you’d want,” he said. “Coffee. Tea. Space from me?”

He said it carefully, like he was already bracing for the answer.

Nick watched him for a moment, then nodded. “Coffee’s good.”

The kettle began to warm, a low hum filling the space between them.

“You’re acting like I’ll want you to leave,” Nick said.

Mike inhaled. “I—”

“Stop.”

The word landed gently, but it held.

Nick met his eyes, steady and clear. “I know what I saw. I know who you are.”  
A beat.  
“And I’m still here.”

Mike’s jaw tightened. He looked away for half a second, then back.

“That doesn’t mean you have to keep staying with me.”

Nick let the sentence sit there. Let it say what it was really offering.

“I’m not staying with you because I have to,” he said.  
“I’m staying because I want to.”

Mike shook his head slightly, more reflex than disagreement. “You shouldn’t feel like you owe me anything.”

“I don’t.”

The kettle clicked, then settled into a quiet simmer.

“This isn’t about owing you,” Nick said.  
“It’s about choosing you.”

Mike swallowed. “I should have told you sooner. I didn’t. And now—”

“Now I know,” Nick said.  
“And I’m not leaving because of it.”

Mike let out a slow breath, like something tight in his chest had finally loosened. “You don’t know what it’s like to live like this.”

“No,” Nick said.  
Then, more gently, “But I know what it looks like when someone starts pulling away.”

Silence stretched between them — not brittle, not fragile. Just real.

“I’m not asking you to make this easier,” Nick continued.  
“I’m telling you I’m staying with you.”

Mike searched his face, like he was still waiting for the ground to shift beneath his feet.

“…You’re sure?”

“Yes.”

The kettle clicked off, steam curling faintly upward.

Mike nodded once.  
“…Then stay.”  
“Stay with me.”

Nick didn’t hesitate. “I’m not going anywhere, Bear.”

Mike smiled — small, unguarded, real.

“Thanks, Cub.”
