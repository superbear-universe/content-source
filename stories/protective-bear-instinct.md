The apartment felt too quiet.

Mike stood in the middle of his living room, arms folded tight across his chest, staring at nothing in particular. The city outside still moved—sirens in the distance, traffic humming—but in here everything had stalled, like the world was holding its breath and refusing to exhale with him.

A few hours ago, Nick had left.

Not angrily.  
Not hurt.  
Just… confused.

Which was worse.

Mike squeezed his eyes shut and scrubbed a hand down his face, fingers catching in his beard. He could still see it: Nick’s smile earlier that night, soft and hopeful, the way he’d laughed at something stupid Mike had said. The way he’d leaned in without thinking, like closeness was easy. Natural.

Like it didn’t scare the hell out of him.

Because it had been lovely. That was the problem.

Mike hadn’t been pretending. Not about the warmth, the ease, the way being with Nick made him feel lighter—more *present*. He’d felt it settling in hours ago, that quiet, dangerous realization that this wasn’t just a date anymore. That this was becoming something that could matter. Something he could lose.

“I can’t,” he muttered to the empty room.

He wanted to tell Nick. God, he wanted to. The truth sat there like an obvious solution, heavy and tempting. *I’m Superbear. This is why I disappear. This is why I hesitate. This is why I pull back.* It would explain everything. It would make the distance make sense instead of cruel.

But wanting didn’t make it safe.

Mike moved to the window, looking down at the street far below. Anyone could be watching. Anyone always could be. That was the reality he lived in now—not paranoia, not drama. Just fact.

He’d learned quickly what happened when heroes got *personal*.

It didn’t take masterminds or supervillains. Just someone patient enough to notice patterns. Someone cruel enough to realize that Superbear—the big, indestructible symbol—had something soft he’d actually bleed for.

Nick.

The thought turned Mike’s stomach.

Nick wasn’t reckless. He wasn’t naïve. But he was open in a way the world punished. If anyone ever connected the dots—if they learned who Mike was, really—Nick wouldn’t be a partner.

He’d be leverage.

“I won’t do that to you,” Mike said quietly, as if Nick could hear him.

That was the line he couldn’t cross.

Distance wasn’t rejection.  
It was containment.

It was the only way Mike knew how to keep the people he cared about from becoming targets.

And that was when he felt it.

The sensation had started weeks ago, subtle enough that he’d tried to convince himself it wasn’t real. Emotional pressure that didn’t belong to him. Impressions that slipped through when his guard was down. Most of the time, he could keep it contained—file it away as background noise, another side effect of powers he was still learning to live with.

But strong emotions made it harder.

And tonight, everything was exposed.

Nick’s feelings brushed against the edge of Mike’s awareness, uninvited and impossible to ignore. Not thoughts. Not images. Just emotion—muted by distance, blurred and indistinct, but unmistakably *his*.

Disappointment.  
Confusion.  
That quiet, circling ache of trying to understand something that wouldn’t explain itself.

Mike’s jaw tightened.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/d087ae950c2f67d297ea03f39705c562/b4ca1907b93ed246-32/s640x960/a5a47bb486616e3d3f94af9b901f51083ae7bf27.png)

“Stop,” he whispered, though he wasn’t sure who he was speaking to.

He couldn’t feel where Nick was—no direction, no sense of place. Distance only softened the signal, like listening through too many walls at once. Farther away now, but not gone.

It felt like a curse.

*This is exactly why I can’t let this get closer,* he thought grimly.

The feelings shifted.

The disappointment dulled, replaced by restlessness—too much noise, too many bodies, motion layered over unresolved hurt. Someone trying to drown a thought instead of sitting with it.

It lingered. Long enough for Mike to tell himself it would pass.

Then confusion sharpened.

Mike straightened, heart beginning to race.

“What are you doing…?” he murmured.

Shock followed—not fear yet, but the cold jolt of seeing something that didn’t fit. Something that felt *wrong* in a way that couldn’t be reasoned away.

Then—

Fear.

Raw. Immediate. A violent spike of dread that tore through him without warning. The kind that came from being *noticed*. From realizing too late that you were no longer unseen.

“No—”

The sensation vanished.

Not fading.  
Not easing.

Gone.

The absence hit harder than the fear itself—a clean, terrifying silence where Nick’s presence had been moments before. Mike’s instincts flared instantly, every part of him reacting before thought could catch up.

Nick hadn’t walked away.

Nick hadn’t calmed down.

Nick had been cut off.

Nick was *in danger*.

And keeping his distance had just failed.

The silence snapped.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/655d445728f99bed915be50cfc1c879e/b4ca1907b93ed246-bc/s640x960/b8af2dc22c11daee7f82b4cca522c8f0364dcad5.png)

“Crap—fuck,” Mike blurted, the words tearing out of him before he could stop them.

He paced once, hard, hands fisting at his sides. His heart hammered, breath coming faster than he liked. Panic flared hot and sharp—not the clean urgency he felt on patrol, but something messier. Personal.

He hated it.

He’d thought he had this under control. Thought distance was enough. Thought that if he stayed careful, stayed quiet, stayed *Mike*, nothing would spill over into something he couldn’t fix.

That illusion shattered with the empty space where Nick’s presence had been.

“Okay. Okay,” he muttered, forcing the words out like anchors. “Get it together.”

This wasn’t a problem Mike could solve.

This was a job for Superbear.

The decision settled fast, instinctive and absolute. Mike planted his feet in the middle of the living room and closed his eyes. He’d learned this part by accident, too—another thing that had revealed itself only when he stopped fighting it.

He focused inward.

On the warmth.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/8ea25f597d83fd877e53899b3f9a451e/b4ca1907b93ed246-ae/s640x960/d2bd5beaae67ff2118f4fe4d35aaa7ee99b76d92.png)

It was always there now, coiled beneath his skin like a second heartbeat. He gathered it deliberately, drawing it up through his chest, his shoulders, his arms. The heat built, bright and humming, flooding his body with familiar pressure. The air around him prickled.

“Alright,” he breathed. “Let’s go.”

He released.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/615a957395a1b9d13749ecc1b6bbb39c/b4ca1907b93ed246-7a/s640x960/90649734df036e51ac0e0d5f340995296b16c9c5.png)

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/6e965e729066647a490ab9a74d92fb1d/b4ca1907b93ed246-6c/s640x960/c3cc70a848c51c1404a8dcf67f65eb794f81fe2b.png)

Rainbow light burst outward in a sudden, blinding wash, filling the apartment with color. The glow rolled over him in waves, reshaping, settling, locking into place with the ease of something that already knew where it belonged.

The light snapped off.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/bb93bdd4d729f0840edd4777781a5a76/b4ca1907b93ed246-d3/s640x960/dfec0462edfd9932f5a38314836a97e3e7d81438.png)

Superbear stood where Mike had been—broad, solid, cape settling behind him as the room returned to normal, suddenly too small again.

No time.

Finding Nick came next.

Superbear turned toward the couch, zeroing in on the rumpled blanket Nick had pulled around himself earlier. He grabbed it and brought it up, inhaling deeply.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/24eb60dc1394a5be11893ff712401c0f/b4ca1907b93ed246-a6/s640x960/06923ad593a7e6e742b53a916cace8b47c8235ed.png)

Nick.

The scent hit immediately—warm fabric, soap, something distinctly *him* beneath it all. Superbear’s senses sharpened around it, the world narrowing as his focus locked on that single thread. He followed it instinctively, letting it guide him instead of overthinking it.

There.

A faint trail, drifting outward.

Superbear moved to the window, threw it open, and leaned out into the night air. The scent was thinner here, stretched and scattered—but still there, carried along streets and rooftops by currents he could read now without trying.

“Hang on, Cub,” he said, voice tight.

Then he launched himself into the sky, cape snapping as rainbow light streaked behind him, already tracking the trail Nick had left behind.
