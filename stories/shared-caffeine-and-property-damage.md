***Nick meets a suspiciously strong bear of a guy…***

Nick noticed him before he meant to.

It wasn’t the size, at least not at first. It was the way the man *hesitated* at the counter, shoulders drawn in slightly, like he was trying to remember how much space he was allowed to take up. The coffee shop was busy in its usual mid-morning way, soft chatter, cups clinking, the low hiss of steaming milk—but around him there was a subtle disruption, as if the room was unconsciously making room.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/2f6a80bf59a74d7c2a61162f1c2cfd95/8c61e199c51872a7-d0/s640x960/2667cb3da19245ea879beb4d1549d4e359e55463.png)

The guy wore a grey T-shirt that had clearly belonged to a smaller version of himself. It stretched tight across a broad chest and thick arms, the fabric pulled almost translucent in places. His jeans did their best to cooperate but failed honestly, strained across heavy thighs that shifted with awkward care. Black-rimmed glasses sat a little crooked on his nose, doing their job *too well*. They made him look gentler, more approachable, almost shy.

Nick tried not to stare.

Then the counter *dented*.

It wasn’t loud. That was the strange part. No crack, no splintering. Just a low, compressed thump as the man rested both hands on the wooden surface while leaning forward to order.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/2d123b8056522d69c566a6b5ac86e6f9/8c61e199c51872a7-dc/s640x960/434ddab3798558edff91520e37f9aaa2b2728314.png)

The barista froze.

So did the man.

His eyes widened behind the glasses. He lifted his hands immediately, like a kid caught touching something fragile.

“Oh, sorry. I… sorry,” he blurted out, already fumbling backward. “I didn’t mean to… I mean, I wasn’t…”

“It’s okay,” the barista said, though she was staring at the shallow hand-shaped impressions in the wood. “Uh. Happens. Sometimes.”

Nick almost laughed. Almost.

The man nodded too quickly, cheeks flushing beneath his beard. He tucked his hands into his pockets as if that might solve everything and waited for his coffee with the intense focus of someone diffusing a bomb.

Nick realized, distantly, that he’d been coming to this coffee shop for weeks.

And that this was the first time he’d *really* seen him.

When the man stepped aside with his cup, carefully holding it with both hands like it might explode, Nick felt the familiar internal argument begin. *Don’t. You don’t do this. You don’t talk to strangers. Especially not… this kind of stranger.*

Except the stranger was now standing awkwardly near the condiment bar, clearly unsure what to do next, shoulders tense with restraint rather than confidence.

Nick moved before he could think himself out of it.

“Hey,” he said, immediately hating how small his voice sounded.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/7b5f080f8ba33b5bf78ea88d14859d11/8c61e199c51872a7-65/s640x960/47c230972392cf97997dbd367a2df46db1611d85.png)

The man turned, startled. Up close, he was… a lot. Not in an aggressive way. In a *contained* way. Like a dam still learning its own limits.

“Hi,” the man replied. He smiled, quick and nervous. “Uh, sorry if I… about the counter. I swear I wasn’t trying to…”

“No, I…” Nick gestured vaguely behind him. “It was kind of impressive. In a… concerning way.”

That earned a soft, embarrassed laugh.

“Yeah. I’m still working on… things.”

They stood there for half a second too long.

Nick took a breath. Felt his heart pound in his ears. Decided to be brave in the smallest way possible.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/09721012fdd1e0db8032add6823e943c/8c61e199c51872a7-cb/s640x960/6a8c1ad06fac15c9e98c38d02d072b6a7f8738a7.png)

“I’m Nick,” he said. “I’ve seen you here a few times.”

“Oh. Yeah. I mean, me too. I mean, I’ve seen *you*.” The man winced. “That came out wrong. I’m Mike.”

Mike.

That fit.

Nick nodded toward the empty table by the window. “I was about to sit. If you want. No pressure. Just coffee.”

Mike looked at the chair like it might not survive him. Then back at Nick.

“I’d like that,” he said carefully. “I promise I’ll be gentle with the furniture.”

Nick smiled, warm and real this time.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/eba77e429a51785b169c4218fb28c36d/8c61e199c51872a7-6e/s640x960/61a7d515601ea7e3d8a5580c3063ee2ea89148c0.png)

As they walked over, Mike moved with deliberate slowness, hyper-aware of every step, every shift of weight. Nick noticed how often Mike checked his own hands, like he didn’t quite trust them yet.

It was oddly endearing.

When they sat, the chair held. The table stayed intact. The world didn’t end.

Nick felt something settle in his chest.

“So,” he said, wrapping his fingers around his cup. “Do you come here often?”

Mike huffed a laugh. “I’m trying to. It helps me feel… normal.”

Nick met his eyes, really met them this time.

“Yeah,” he said softly. “Me too.”

And for a moment, amid the hum of the coffee shop and the quiet restraint of impossible strength, something small and real began to take shape.

—

For a few quiet minutes, things felt almost… normal.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/08c9d49fb19c9103d4cf29b0fae4e3c6/8c61e199c51872a7-ba/s640x960/faa8114c7889a93fd658aaf5f48104e3f5a76e3c.png)

They talked about nothing important. Which pastries were overrated. How the coffee here was better than it had any right to be. Mike relaxed enough to lean back in his chair, then immediately corrected himself, sitting upright again, apologizing under his breath for no discernible reason.

Nick watched him with an easy fondness that surprised him.

Eventually, Nick took a breath and decided not to overthink it.

“So,” he said, casual but deliberate. “This might be awkward. But I’d regret not asking.”

Mike’s attention snapped fully to him.

“Would you want to go out sometime?” Nick continued. “Like an actual date. Not just… shared caffeine and property damage.”

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/03ea2717ec0aa24e15dfe72d93fab981/8c61e199c51872a7-fe/s640x960/57f7c05b4c59ee42e71b6e93a3f691b02f3959d2.png)

For half a second, Mike looked genuinely alarmed.

His brain stalled. His shoulders went stiff. His hands clenched around his cup a little too hard.

“Oh,” he said.

Nick immediately added, “You can say no. I really mean that.”

“No… I mean…” Mike shook his head, words tripping over each other. “I want to. I just… yes. Yes, I want to.”

He nodded too fast, then laughed at himself, cheeks warm beneath his beard.

“Yes,” he repeated, steadier this time. “I’d really like that.”

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/6a1b9e708eeeda78c0f48a557141cf0b/8c61e199c51872a7-7b/s640x960/bf6dd06221d23521b02ce25565244c7ff5ab171b.png)

Nick smiled, relief and excitement hitting at once.

“Good,” he said. “Because I was hoping you’d say yes.”

They exchanged numbers, both of them checking their phones like the contact might evaporate if they didn’t look directly at it. Mike stared at Nick’s name for a second longer than necessary, then tucked his phone away carefully.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/53899caa7e6d2f7898c0fece3302b4df/8c61e199c51872a7-61/s640x960/f55e4f15d0c82abfe33994a013fda7d0c5c2cd67.png)

That was when the coffee shop TV cut in.

The chatter dipped as a breaking news banner filled the screen.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/1a706bd30bc2763263a1d805d44e0552/8c61e199c51872a7-8a/s640x960/4e7b895a4aaf6e8c8b839bff8c59c253c6dfe0c0.png)

> **“A developing situation on Delmar Street, where a fire has broken out in a residential building. Emergency crews are en route, but residents may still be trapped inside.”**

Smoke poured from the upper floors on-screen. People clustered below, pointing, shouting. Sirens wailed faintly through the speakers.

Nick felt a knot form in his stomach. “That’s awful.”

When he turned back, Mike had already pushed his chair back.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/1709fc3dc36e0f150a1e71f8dd5a67e1/8c61e199c51872a7-69/s640x960/7975edc2dc6966f434bfb9c44e00f26151f39c21.png)

“I’m… I’m sorry,” Mike said quickly. Too quickly. “I have to go.”

Nick blinked. “Right now?”

“Yeah. Something just- came up.” Mike hesitated, like he was weighing words and choosing none of them. “I didn’t mean for today to end like this.”

“It’s okay,” Nick said, confused but not upset. “Just text me?”

Mike’s face softened instantly.

“I will,” he said. “I promise.”

Then he was gone, out the door and into the street, moving fast, leaving Nick standing there with cooling coffee and unanswered questions.

A few seconds later, the coffee shop erupted.

Nick turned just in time to see the live feed change.

A massive figure in blue and red descended through the smoke, landing in front of the burning building with impossible weight and grace. A cape snapped in the heat as **Superbear** surged forward, vanishing into the flames.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/94a51c4cf66733b6519aba9430966d43/8c61e199c51872a7-24/s640x960/a09fb598940c9e16f27e6f79375cf38ff2d2ab8c.png)

Gasps. Phones raised. Cheers mixed with fear.

Nick stared at the screen, awe overtaking everything else.

“Wow,” he breathed.

He slipped his phone into his pocket, thumb brushing over Mike’s name once, unconsciously.

Outside, sirens screamed. On-screen, Superbear re-emerged with someone cradled safely in his arms.

Nick smiled, equal parts shaken and quietly happy.

“Well,” he murmured to himself, “that was… a lot for a first almost-date.”

Still completely oblivious.
