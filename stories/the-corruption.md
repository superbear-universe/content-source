Nick had learned the shape of Mike’s couch.

Not just where the cushions dipped, but how they fit there together — Nick tucked easily under Mike’s arm, Mike shifting without thought to make room. The TV filled the apartment with soft light and half-heard dialogue, something familiar enough that neither of them was really watching anymore.

This had become normal.

Nick kicked his shoes off without asking, draped his jacket over the chair, reached for the blanket at the end of the couch like it belonged to him. Mike’s arm rested around his shoulders, solid and warm, hand relaxed against his upper arm. Nick leaned into it without thinking, cheek against Mike’s chest, listening to his steady breathing.

He felt safe.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/97495527a71485295dce8baffd5938fd/9288a8d7af1c8fb1-76/s640x960/cbd9c7328fcb8dc431f9c5284ae3f50edf4170be.png)

Mike’s hand stayed where it always did. When Nick shifted, Mike adjusted automatically, careful and attentive, tucking the blanket higher, brushing a kiss against Nick’s hair. Affectionate. Gentle.

Contained.

They always stopped here.

It wasn’t obvious at first — just a pattern Nick had slowly learned, the way you learn the limits of something by brushing up against them enough times. Weeks of easy dates and shared evenings, warmth and laughter and quiet care.

Nick had stopped expecting more *tonight*.

When the show ended, Nick stretched slightly and tilted his head back. “I could just stay over,” he said lightly. “If you want. It’s late anyway.”

It was an invitation, not a challenge. He didn’t even look at Mike when he said it.

Mike went still for half a second.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/d3d78a488407b9260f1e3221dc493639/9288a8d7af1c8fb1-90/s640x960/5b6c49e48a86682649268a046de3172e7b6b1c3d.png)

Then he smiled — a beat too late. “I’ve got an early thing tomorrow,” he said gently. “I don’t want you to feel rushed.”

Nick nodded immediately. “Yeah. Of course.”

Mike squeezed his arm. “Next time.”

“Next time,” Nick echoed, smiling back.

They sat together a little longer, conversation drifting back to nothing. Nick laughed when Mike said something stupid, rested against him again like everything was fine.

Inside, something settled into place.

This wasn’t timing. It wasn’t mood. It wasn’t uncertainty.

This was a line Mike wouldn’t cross.

Nick didn’t know why, and because he didn’t know, he filled the gap himself. *Maybe he needs more time. Maybe I’m moving too fast.* He didn’t push. He didn’t ask for explanations Mike clearly couldn’t give.

At the door, Mike helped him into his jacket, kissed him softly — lingering, careful, stopping just short like always.

“Text me when you get home.”

“I will.”

Outside, the night air felt sharper than it should have.

Nick walked a block before the frustration surfaced — brief and hot, followed by confusion, then the quieter ache underneath it. He pulled out his phone without thinking.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/029fc8b149869111e4ea7a8a4693dda5/9288a8d7af1c8fb1-ca/s640x960/985bbb1607335e2adfbd3213c863af0751a5cc9e.png)

A message waited for him.

*Evan: We’ve got an extra ticket if you want to come. Black Ursa’s playing tonight.*

Nick stared at it.

He didn’t think he was running from Mike. He just knew he couldn’t sit with the feeling he’d carried out of that apartment.

*Yeah,* he typed. *I’m in.*

He slipped the phone away and turned toward the city lights, toward noise and bodies and somewhere loud enough to drown out the question pressing at the back of his mind.

*Why won’t you let me in?*

# - - -

The warehouse sat low and wide at the edge of the old dockyards, rusted steel and concrete vibrating with sound. The bass reached Nick before the door did, a physical pressure that climbed his legs and settled in his chest as he stepped inside.

Evan was already there, grinning, hair damp with sweat.

“You made it,” he shouted, clapping Nick on the shoulder. “They’re about to start.”

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/41deb9e6819e53a57206514211314777/9288a8d7af1c8fb1-6e/s640x960/9db38508a4075e68700daab13a5c7328144b8572.png)

Nick barely had time to shout something back before the crowd surged, bodies shifting as the lights dropped and Black Ursa tore into the space. The sound swallowed everything — guitars screaming, drums hammering hard enough to rattle bone. Heat, beer, motion. Too much in exactly the way Nick needed.

He let it take him.

Evan disappeared toward the bar during the first song, raising his hand in a vague be right back gesture that meant nothing once the room compressed again. Nick lost sight of him almost immediately, swallowed by the crowd as it surged and reformed around the stage.

It didn’t matter. Not yet.

Nick watched the band instead — the confidence in their stance, the way the singer held the mic like it owed him something. There was no hesitation here. No stopping short. Just volume and presence and permission to take up space.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/989cc79292206006fae0462e510fce47/9288a8d7af1c8fb1-20/s640x960/c7df27ba1808b8bb2f77933eb02f8dfdee096488.png)

His shoulders loosened. His thoughts went quiet.

Only later, between songs, did he start to notice the men.

They were scattered through the crowd, not clustered. Big men. Thick arms, heavy beards. Several wore leather harnesses over their shirts, straps cutting clean lines across chests that didn’t move much at all.

They weren’t dancing. They weren’t shouting lyrics.

They stood still, watching.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/4e5499828f4baf7aaa7627571a0b691e/9288a8d7af1c8fb1-66/s640x960/d24f3a432e0f823dada9987e13c239a2279802ba.png)

Nick clocked them without knowing why. Another subculture, he told himself. Another pocket of the night he didn’t quite belong to.

During a break in the set, he noticed one of them leaning close to a smaller man. Not touching him exactly — just standing within his space. The smaller man nodded as if being told something important, shoulders easing as though something had settled.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/12bc16a19cbf43faff8c10db2da86d1e/9288a8d7af1c8fb1-98/s640x960/1ee724cb20c340bbbab75374ebd68aaa9710a597.png)

The music roared back to life before Nick could think too much about it.

He checked his phone. No messages. He scanned the crowd for Evan’s jacket, his hair, anything familiar.

Nothing.

*He probably went to the bar*, Nick thought. *Or the bathroom.*

The thought pulled him toward the edge of the room, where the sound softened and the lights dimmed. A narrow corridor ran along the side of the building, concrete walls sweating with condensation, the music muffled but still present.

*I’ll just check, he told himself. If he’s not here, I’ll leave.*

The corridor sloped upward slightly, opening onto a metal staircase Nick hadn’t noticed before. Industrial. Unmarked. Something left over from the building’s old life.

Nick hesitated, then climbed.

At the top, the space opened unexpectedly.

A narrow catwalk ran along the wall, overlooking a lower room set apart from the main floor. The sound dropped away almost completely here, replaced by a heavy quiet that pressed against his ears.

Nick stopped.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/a251c7b983249d695b47fcc3f6b7cdaa/9288a8d7af1c8fb1-4d/s640x960/571568b4367f21eb8d746b1cadca88e3c2e7e098.png)

Below him, the room was lit low and deliberate. Not dark — controlled. Men stood positioned around the space, not crowded, not loose. Waiting. At the center stood a smaller man, his shoulders are bare, posture uncertain but not resistant.

One of the harnessed men stepped close, guiding him with a hand placed flat and steady at his back.

Nick’s breath caught.

He recognized him.

The same face from the crowd. The same man who had nodded, who had looked relieved.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/80a500e2ede0d493692899130ca3f6fc/9288a8d7af1c8fb1-29/s640x960/435b366ead671891f1cfef7e1ec864cd8be13980.png)

Down below, no one rushed him. No one forced anything. The smaller man inhaled deeply, then let the breath go, like he was setting something down. When a harness was lifted, when he reached out to accept it, his hands didn’t shake.

Nick felt cold all at once.

This wasn’t casual. This wasn’t sex. This wasn’t music.

Something was being given up here.

The men below didn’t look up. Their attention was focused inward, on the center of the room, on the process unfolding with quiet certainty. Nick stood frozen on the catwalk, heart pounding, careful not to move, not to breathe too loudly.

He had only meant to check.

And now he knew — not everything, not yet — but enough to understand that he had stepped somewhere he was never meant to be.

The smaller man lifted the harness and drew it over his head.

The leather settled against his chest.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/7cba38e1c754bee4c657ce369587ce49/9288a8d7af1c8fb1-b5/s640x960/4f2f0672723e4e7c7e2aeea49180a60a4a99d474.png)

Nick expected hesitation. A pause. Something.

Instead, the man’s body changed.

Not violently. Not all at once. His chest pressed forward into the straps, shoulders spreading wider as if space were being claimed for him. His stance shifted without instruction, feet planting farther apart, weight dropping lower. The harness drew taut as mass gathered beneath it, the lines no longer decorative but structural.

Dark hair began to creep across his chest. His jaw thickened, face broadening by degrees that were subtle enough to miss if you weren’t watching closely.

He lifted his head again, and something in his expression was gone.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/1c5646dd0c3228111a0342645afe1cc5/9288a8d7af1c8fb1-f0/s640x960/20c4a5f2814982d36687102594297b51e9645900.png)

Nick’s stomach turned cold.

This wasn’t metaphor.

Behind him, something shifted.

Not footsteps. Not a voice. Just the faint displacement of air, close enough that his skin registered it before his mind did.

He started to turn—

Pain bloomed at the base of his skull, dense and overwhelming. Not sharp. Not dramatic. Just enough to steal his balance and collapse the world inward.

The catwalk lurched. The room below smeared into light and shadow as his knees buckled.

He fell, his face hitting the metal.

Then the light went out.
