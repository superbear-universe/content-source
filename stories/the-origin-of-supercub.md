The TV played quietly in the corner of the living room.

Nick hadn’t turned it on. He also hadn’t turned it off.

Daylight filled the apartment in a way the night never had — soft, ordinary, almost careless. Dust drifted through sunbeams on the floor. Outside, the city went about its business, already done caring.

On the screen, shaky footage showed a dockside warehouse bursting apart. Concrete exploded outward, dust and debris flooding the frame as a blue-and-red shape tore through the wall like it wasn’t there.

The anchor’s voice stayed neutral. Bored, even.

> “—earlier this morning, emergency services responded to a disturbance at an industrial site along the riverfront. Witnesses report significant property damage. Authorities have not yet commented on—”

Nick watched the moment Superbear reappeared.

Watched the way he carried someone in his arms.

Just a body, held close and unmoving.

*Unidentified individual*, the caption read.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/602cbbc990260708a3e9ab2a3e693177/63634de9019de5e5-0b/s640x960/7ef706cbc5f88e1f6b8f6485333c756f68fddcd5.png)

Mike stood near the kitchen counter, arms folded tight across his chest. He wasn’t really watching — not directly. His eyes flicked toward the screen and away again, jaw clenched, shoulders locked.

Nick said nothing.

After a few seconds too many, Mike reached out and turned the TV off.

The silence rang.

Neither of them moved.

The quiet wasn’t fragile. It wasn’t awkward. It was the kind that settled after something real had already happened — something that couldn’t be undone.

Nick leaned back against the sofa cushions, staring at the blank screen.

“That wasn’t an accident,” he said.

Mike inhaled sharply. “Nick—”

“They didn’t take me by accident.”

The words were calm. Factual.

Mike turned toward him fully. “You don’t know that.”

Nick nodded once. “I do.”

Memory clicked into place — not the pain, not the restraints, but the voice. Calm. Certain.

*You just walked into his.*

“They didn’t need me awake,” Nick continued. “They didn’t need to hurt me. They didn’t even need me conscious.” He met Mike’s eyes. “They needed you reckless.”

Mike went pale.

“They needed a reason you wouldn’t ignore,” Nick said. “A reason you couldn’t plan around. Something you’d tear the world apart to get back.”

Mike took a step toward him without meaning to, hands flexing uselessly. “Nick, I—”

“This isn’t a one-off,” Nick said gently. “And it’s not going to stop.”

The truth settled between them, heavy and undeniable.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/fff95a5c49864ffc922c5bbc627a7150/63634de9019de5e5-cc/s640x960/132275ffd921393dfea11771d1acc013e977ae53.png)

“I can protect you,” Mike said too quickly.

Nick shook his head. “Not like this.”

“I can be more careful.”

“You can’t be everywhere.”

Mike opened his mouth, then closed it again. Panic crept into his posture — small, but visible.

“Living under constant protection,” Nick said quietly, “is still living in a cage.”

Mike dragged a hand through his hair. “Then what am I supposed to do?”

The words slipped out before he could stop them.

“Unless you were—”  
“Unless I— no.”

He froze.

Nick tilted his head. “…If I were like you.”

Mike recoiled immediately. “No. I shouldn’t have said that. That’s not something you suggest. That’s not something you decide for someone else.”

“I know,” Nick said.

He stood and crossed the room, stopping directly in front of Mike. Close enough now that Mike had to look at him.

“You’re talking like this would be something you’d do *to* me,” Nick said. “I’m talking about something I’d choose.”

Mike stared at him, fear naked in his eyes. “You don’t know what you’re asking.”

“Then tell me.”

So Mike did.

He explained slowly. Carefully. Like every word carried weight.

That it was irreversible. That it changed the body permanently. That it meant danger, visibility, responsibility. That once it began, it did not stop.

Nick listened. Asked questions. Didn’t rush.

When Mike finished, Nick nodded.

“I’m not choosing power,” Nick said. “I’m choosing not to disappear.”

Silence stretched.

Mike searched his face for doubt.

There wasn’t any.

“I know what this means,” Nick said. “I know it changes everything.”

Mike swallowed.

“I’m choosing this,” Nick continued. “I’m choosing you. And I’m choosing to stand.”

Mike let out a slow breath.

“Okay,” he said quietly.

He didn’t touch Nick right away.

He waited.

Nick nodded once.

That was enough.

Mike stepped forward and pulled Nick into a hug.

A full bear hug — arms around Nick’s shoulders and back, chest pressed flush. Grounded. The world narrowing down to contact and breath and the certainty of being held.

Then the air changed.

A faint rainbow glow gathered around Mike first — subtle at the edges, alive — and seeped into Nick through every point of contact.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/d95b0da7080b72edc1ad35620560af1f/63634de9019de5e5-c0/s640x960/8c94737ee811ee22c585b994f2db017a4e5c1155.png)

Warmth bloomed deep behind Nick’s sternum.

His breath caught — then deepened. His lungs expanded physically, giving him more room to exist inside himself. The heat slid down his spine, loosening tension he hadn’t known was there.

Pressure built beneath his skin.

Not pain.

**Inevitability.**

Nick’s shoulders widened first.

He felt the pull deep in bone and muscle as mass arrived steadily. His shirt tightened across his upper back. His chest followed — pectorals swelling forward, heavy and dense, pressing against Mike’s chest.

Nick gasped.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/2e20618015d6baf223202e5ba2b309f3/63634de9019de5e5-ea/s640x960/ecf13290ed49a2a673908a649789189fe747e9bc.png)

Mike’s arms tightened reflexively for half a heartbeat—

—and then he stopped.

He felt Nick pushing outward in his hold.

And he let go.

He released Nick carefully, on purpose, hands sliding down his sides as he stepped back half a pace.

So he could see.

Nick stood there, breathing hard, warmth still spreading through him in heavy waves.

Mike stared.

His eyes tracked Nick’s body as it changed — worry tight across his mouth, awe bright behind his eyes.

“Nick…” he breathed. Not a warning. Just his name.

Nick’s shoulders broadened further, rounding into thickness that made his frame look *built* for strength. His arms followed — biceps swelling heavy and full, triceps rounding into dense mass.

His sleeves strained.

Ripped.

Nick looked down just in time to see fabric tear open along his upper arm.

Another rip followed.

He flexed without thinking.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/66a301e650a122ddd62e6dc613cef894/63634de9019de5e5-fc/s640x960/277af0e87a041e95436aa18001d315fbea3d10a0.png)

The movement felt effortless. Powerful.

Mike made a sound under his breath — half laugh, half stunned exhale.

“You’re—” Mike stopped, swallowed hard. “You’re okay?”

Nick inhaled again, deeper than before, and nodded. “Yeah. I’m okay.”

Heat pooled across Nick’s core.

His stomach pushed outward, muscle and weight arriving together — firm beneath a soft layer that grounded him. His stance widened instinctively as his thighs thickened into heavy columns. His jeans dug in, then protested.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/d2a940e33e1a1010b60938b1446e3d3e/63634de9019de5e5-5c/s640x960/7414714553bcb235263b1b3af071bf14ed7b0be1.png)

The apartment creaked.

Mike’s gaze flicked down and back up again.

He looked scared.

He also looked *thrilled*.

Like part of him wanted to rush forward and steady Nick — and part of him couldn’t believe the world was letting him watch this happen.

Hair spread across Nick’s chest and stomach, warmth prickling intimately. His jaw tingled as his beard filled in thick beneath his palm.

Nick laughed, breathless.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/d0796b210d5e624e8938b13af686208d/63634de9019de5e5-a7/s640x960/0f695bfb2ab3116dff1256a3fb5e2bdc50fedff5.png)

Mike stared at him like something was breaking open in his chest.

“Oh my god,” he whispered.

The warmth surged again.

Rainbow light intensified, washing the walls in color. The air hummed faintly.

Nick’s feet lifted from the floor.

Just an inch.

Then more.

Nick startled and laughed at the same time.

Mike’s breath caught — excitement flickering into fear for a heartbeat — but he didn’t tell Nick to stop.

He just watched.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/f86e3f0dd4c2d1268e58c53c5d5fd60d/63634de9019de5e5-4f/s640x960/74c18e068ddf9c1959b8f3fd3e5669d6b83c6b8c.png)

The rainbow light surged.

For a heartbeat, it felt like the world folded around Nick instead of away from him. Color thickened until it wasn’t just light anymore but *pressure* — against his skin, his chest, his teeth. The hum in the air deepened, vibrating through his bones until it was impossible to tell where sound ended and sensation began.

Nick’s vision washed white.

Not blank — *full*.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/db310143bc048714ace6ae2364d4cf85/63634de9019de5e5-d9/s640x960/18d6b8e497367d149f90bc55e11059b8e89a044d.png)

Every nerve in his body lit up at once, heat and weight and awareness colliding in a single, overwhelming instant. His heart slammed hard enough that he felt it in his throat. His breath hitched, stalled — and then forced its way back in, deeper than any breath he’d ever taken.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/15986b19b7e1103cef1651250a8ef643/63634de9019de5e5-ac/s640x960/73955212f7a7d447173967a2cc7fa625876bcc73.png)

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/ba2ad15ae4255cd58ca19a768f108e42/63634de9019de5e5-ae/s640x960/a0c18f9dbd63a14aca91691be062c61bf31d56b1.png)

Something *locked*.

It was the unmistakable sensation of completion — like the last piece of something immense settling into place. His body stopped *changing* and started *being*.

The pressure eased.

The sound fell away in layers, like the world remembering how to exist normally again. Color thinned, light pulling back from solid presence to glow, from glow to shimmer, from shimmer to nothing at all.

Nick felt himself descend.

Not dropped — *returned*.

His boots met the floor with a weight that felt permanent, undeniable. The apartment answered with a soft, complaining creak, as if acknowledging that this version of him wasn’t going anywhere.

Silence followed.

Nick stood there, breath steadying, heart still pounding — aware of every inch of himself in a way that felt new and absolute.

He was done becoming.

He *was*.

Nick looked down.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/c73d5c95755a0e70e7d1d6f1f242af5d/63634de9019de5e5-f6/s640x960/7438b96eb6717f71085f77705708998d7b9e4095.png)

His ruined clothes were gone — replaced by a blue-and-yellow suit that fit his new body perfectly, a red cape resting against his back, a red belt framing his solid midsection.

He shifted his weight—

And hovered.

Mike’s feet left the floor a second later.

Not because he meant to.

Because his body followed Nick’s.

They floated there, face to face, the apartment floor far below them now, suddenly too small to matter.

Mike reached out, hands resting at Nick’s sides — not holding. Just touching.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/e34061117220df47519bfded88175ddf/63634de9019de5e5-ce/s640x960/ee89773473cbaa007d38a35fba9846dac6cc4338.png)

Nick looked at him, something soft and certain settling in his chest.

“Still here,” Nick said quietly.

Mike laughed, breathless and wrecked. “Yeah. You really are.”

Mike hovered there, still looking stunned.

Not scared anymore.

Just… undone.

He reached out slowly, like Nick might vanish if he moved too fast, and set his hands at Nick’s sides. Not to hold him up — Nick didn’t need that — but to hold him because he couldn’t not.

His fingers flexed once, testing the new solidity of him.

The breadth. The weight. The realness.

Mike’s gaze flicked over Nick’s suit — the blue and yellow stretched over a body that filled space like it belonged there — and something bright and helpless broke across his face.

Pride.

A sound left him, half laugh, half breath, like the feeling was too big to stay contained.

“Look at you,” Mike whispered.

Nick’s chest tightened. He barely managed, “Yeah?”

Mike didn’t answer with words.

He lifted one hand and cupped Nick’s jaw, thumb brushing into the thick beard like he needed the texture under his skin to believe it. His palm slid higher, cradling Nick’s cheek with a tenderness that didn’t match the sheer strength in his fingers.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/67ff331f980ebf3ea7da0719fdc45921/63634de9019de5e5-67/s640x960/a392cd0b586e46a28be64f38dfefd287ff44ac05.png)

Nick leaned into the touch without thinking.

Mike’s eyes dragged over his face — over the beard, the heavier lines, the new steadiness — and then met Nick’s again.

Warm. Full. Burning.

“I’m so proud of you,” Mike said, voice rough.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/864f8604db593e112584cbeef757d79d/63634de9019de5e5-07/s640x960/71901b5f8148c115c9be1aae143e997325784f57.png)

Nick swallowed, breath catching. “Bear—”

That was all he got out.

Mike pulled him in.

This time, he didn’t hold back.

His arms wrapped around Nick’s shoulders and back, drawing him flush, chest to chest, the space between them erased completely. Nick felt the contact everywhere — the heat of Mike’s body, the press of Mike’s hands, the way being held by him felt different now that Nick could match it.

Nick’s hands found Mike automatically, gripping him hard — not careful, not tentative — fingers digging into shirt fabric and solid muscle underneath, anchoring them together midair like the world had nothing else worth holding onto.

Mike’s mouth found his.

The kiss hit full.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/c9c288173d21193fddf832cdd84f42a5/63634de9019de5e5-68/s640x960/183a161fab4257836a3320ea1f5b3cfbc0ecc201.png)

Warm and hungry and relieved, like every second of fear had turned into heat the moment Nick became undeniable. Mike kissed him like he meant it — deep, breathless, almost laughing into it — and Nick kissed him back just as hard, letting the rush of it roll through him.

Nicks cape drifted around them.

All Nick could feel was Mike.

All Mike could feel was him.

When they finally broke apart, they were still pressed together, foreheads almost touching, both of them breathing hard.

Mike’s hands stayed on Nick’s face, thumbs still in his beard, like he didn’t know how to stop touching him.

“Hi, Cub,” Mike murmured, voice wrecked in the softest way.

Nick let out a low, disbelieving laugh.

He could feel his own chest rising against Mike’s. The weight of his arms around him. The cape drifting behind his shoulders like it belonged there.

He swallowed, eyes bright.

“I guess…” he breathed, and shook his head once like the words couldn’t possibly be real—

“I guess I’m Supercub.”

They rose together.

Upward.

And this time, neither of them disappeared.
