Nick woke to the sensation of being held upright by something that did not care about comfort.

Cold pressed into his back and shoulders. Metal. His wrists were pulled behind him, bound tight enough that his arms ached the moment awareness returned. His head dipped forward, then snapped back as pain flared behind his eyes—sharp, bright, insistent.

He sucked in a breath and immediately regretted it. The air tasted faintly metallic, dry and stale at the back of his throat.

*Okay,* he thought dimly. *That’s bad.*

He forced his eyes open.

The room swam, then steadied. He was sitting in an old industrial chair—steel or aluminum, bolted and scarred from long use. The kind of thing you stacked in storage rooms or fixed in place because comfort had never been part of the design. His ankles were bound too, tight enough that he could feel the pressure through his shoes.

Not tied in a hurry.  
Not tied in anger.

Tied efficiently.

Nick swallowed, wincing as something pulled along his temple, skin tight and sore in a way that suggested the pain had arrived ahead of memory.

*Concert.*  
*Catwalk.*  
*A hand—*

His stomach dropped.

“Hey,” he said hoarsely, testing his voice. It sounded too loud in the quiet. “Hey—”

Nothing answered.

He shifted, testing the restraints. They didn’t budge. The chair didn’t scrape. Whatever anchored it held firm, as if this exact spot had been chosen long before he arrived in it.

That was when the wrongness settled in.

There were no signs of a struggle. No overturned furniture. No blood on the floor. No chaos frozen into place. The room was bare and utilitarian—concrete walls, exposed beams, a single harsh light overhead. Everything felt reset, orderly, as if what had happened to him had already been processed and filed away.

This wasn’t a place where people were held temporarily.

This was a place where things *happened*.

Footsteps echoed somewhere beyond the room.

Nick straightened as much as the chair allowed, pulse spiking. The sound wasn’t hurried. No shouting. No urgency. Just steady movement, approaching at an unbothered pace.

The door opened.

A man stepped inside.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/5bf3756e578245346f889526a0fe7ed1/8f3fb4f02e6785b8-8a/s640x960/c3b1e20fc555000d91635c91c54f86bee1c6ce08.png)

Nick’s first thought was *guard*.

He was big—bigger than anyone Nick had ever seen up close like this. Broad through the shoulders, thick through the chest, built with the kind of mass that didn’t come from effort so much as permanence. He wore a sleeveless black shirt stretched tight across his torso, leather straps crossing his chest in a rigid X and locking together at a metal ring. Tight leather pants. Heavy boots.

He moved with unhurried confidence, like he knew exactly how much space he occupied and expected the room to make room for him.

Something about him tugged at Nick anyway.

Not the size. Not the clothes.

The way he stood, maybe. Balanced. Still. A half-second pause before he moved again, like he was listening for instructions that never needed to be spoken.

Another presence entered behind him.

Nick felt it before he saw it—a subtle pressure shift, like the room had reoriented itself around a new center of gravity. The man who stepped in was massive, even compared to the guard. Thick through the neck and chest, heavy in a way that made the air feel denser around him.

Black latex stretched smoothly over his torso beneath the harness, glossy under the overhead light, catching reflections as he moved. The straps and metal fittings sat where they were meant to, precise and deliberate. Leather pants completed the uniform, worn with the same unselfconscious certainty.

A thick cigar burned low between his fingers, smoke curling lazily toward the ceiling.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/98b575890147914d27385104e3b7af9a/8f3fb4f02e6785b8-d8/s640x960/fb6646844ffc636f2b5908e134ad5effc8df61a5.png)

His dark eyes moved over Nick with open assessment. Not hunger. Not anger.

Curiosity.

“You weren’t expected,” he said.

His voice was deep, even, carrying easily without being raised.

Nick swallowed. “Who—”

“This isn’t how this usually starts,” the man continued, as if Nick hadn’t spoken at all.

He exhaled through his nose, faint irritation creasing his expression.

“But accidents have a way of complicating routines.”

The larger guard stepped closer. Close enough now that Nick could see his face clearly.

The beard was new. Full. Intentionally kept. His face had rounded out, softened in places that had once been sharp—but the eyes—

The eyes were the same.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/f80298f3d9278bc0940ca7f8118d1ef8/8f3fb4f02e6785b8-3b/s640x960/ae477cd4aedbe79a61064baeed4b2ee154ee7c8b.png)

Nick’s breath caught.

“Evan?” The name slipped out before he could stop it. “Evan—what… what did they do to you?”

Evan didn’t react the way Nick expected. No flicker of recognition. No shame. No relief.

He reached down and checked the bindings at Nick’s wrists with efficient care. Gentle. Professional.

“Evan, stop,” Nick said sharply. “Look at me.”

Evan straightened.

“Sir,” he said calmly, “he’s secure.”

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/2d69a1acc1adeb9fcc79e9694d80f6d2/8f3fb4f02e6785b8-88/s640x960/6f4bffc103f9f018addc3d88a29aeeb8afa0b648.png)

The word hit harder than the restraints ever had.

The man with the cigar glanced briefly at Evan, then back to Nick.

“You spend enough time around a man who flies in full view of the city,” he said mildly, “people start noticing.”

Nick frowned, disoriented. His head throbbed dully now, the pain spreading outward in slow waves. “What are you talking about?”

“No mask. No discipline,” the man went on, irritation threading into his tone. “All that power, worn like a costume. It was only a matter of time before he left fingerprints everywhere.”

Nick shook his head. “I don’t know him. I’ve never even—”

“I don’t bother him,” the man continued, unconcerned. “Too loud. Too strong. Too eager for an audience.”

Smoke drifted between them, undisturbed.

“But everyone has a soft spot.”

Evan adjusted the chair behind Nick, squaring it with practiced ease. He didn’t meet Nick’s eyes.

“You just walked into his,” the man said.

Nick’s chest felt tight. None of this made sense. His body ached in places he hadn’t noticed before, pain blooming slowly now that the shock was wearing off.

“I don’t belong here,” he said. “You’ve got the wrong person.”

The man studied him for a moment longer, then nodded once.

“Maybe,” he said. “But you walked into the room.”

He turned away.

“For now,” he added calmly, “that’s enough.”

Something crashed somewhere in the building.

The sound was distant but unmistakable—metal screaming, concrete fracturing, followed by shouts that cut off abruptly. Evan’s head snapped toward the door.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/f1ddb32ebeafdca08fb9fdfdc545364b/8f3fb4f02e6785b8-6d/s640x960/efd7a2255658207dfc607e40d202ea742f4ad917.png)

Another impact, closer this time. The floor vibrated beneath the chair, rattling up through Nick’s spine.

The door *exploded* inward.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/2fdbc39fe1a62028747cc40749718e52/8f3fb4f02e6785b8-df/s640x960/1a2864fba11276176e27cab77383b8c45aa0787d.png)

Bodies flew past the doorway, slamming into the far wall hard enough to leave dents in the concrete. A massive blue-and-red figure filled the opening a heartbeat later.

Superbear.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/75967f1d8c869d3b78bb373ea741cb78/8f3fb4f02e6785b8-dc/s640x960/03349a5afd4096b4c21161889966f52b4fdd0075.png)

He moved like a force of nature given direction—fast, overwhelming, precise. He didn’t look at the man with the cigar. Didn’t acknowledge him at all.

His eyes locked on Nick.

“Nick.”

Evan stepped forward, one hand reaching for the restraints.

Superbear hit him with a shoulder as he passed—harder than necessary. Evan was knocked off balance and sent sprawling across the concrete, the impact loud and final enough that he didn’t immediately get back up.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/c7abece80e079777548277318419f5d3/8f3fb4f02e6785b8-dc/s640x960/65493ad7ecd74ff0a06e51b177cba4566e3633dd.png)

Superbear was already at the chair.

The restraints snapped apart as if they were nothing.

Nick gasped as circulation rushed back into his wrists. Strong hands steadied him immediately, adjusting their grip with careful precision that made Nick suddenly aware of how much his body hurt.

“I’ve got you,” Superbear said, low and certain.

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/cb367e4297ce3c3387c2764a8abbbcb0/8f3fb4f02e6785b8-11/s640x960/889b414d4a38f3d1519cd20952190cb28e8306e1.png)

Nick looked up at him—and something twisted in his chest.

Not recognition. Not exactly.

Just a wrong, familiar closeness. The shape of his eyes. The way his voice softened when he spoke to him.

Nick’s head swam.

Superbear didn’t give him time to think.

He lifted Nick easily, one arm braced behind his back, the other under his legs. Nick clutched at his suit without meaning to, fingers digging into the fabric as the room tilted violently.

Behind them, the man with the cigar watched, expression unreadable.

“Careful,” he said mildly. “You’re making a lot of noise.”

![](https://64.media.tumblr.com/1d6a66e86bc6f017c19851ddf09f94bc/8f3fb4f02e6785b8-fe/s640x960/9a84a5fbadf6306ac8ee81d3c1edbea7e9d18759.png)

Superbear didn’t slow or turn for the door.

The wall behind them gave way in a roar of shattered concrete and displaced air as he launched into the night.

Cold rushed over Nick’s face as they burst into open space. The city spread beneath them in a wash of lights and motion, distant and unreal.

Nick buried his face against Superbear’s chest, heart racing, breath coming in short, uneven pulls. Strong arms tightened around him, holding him steady as they flew.

Safe.

For now.

But as the building fell away behind them, the man’s words echoed stubbornly in Nick’s head.

*You just walked into his.*

Nick closed his eyes, trying to push the thought away.

Whatever that place was—whatever those people were—

Accident hadn’t protected him.
